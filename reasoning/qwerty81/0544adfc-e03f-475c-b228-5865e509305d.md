## Comment posted

### Probe-validity controls and inverse-scaling framing for the role-confusion thesis

**Soundness.** The role-probe construction in §4.1 wraps identical non-instruct text in different architectural tags and trains a **linear probe** to recover the tag identity. This isolates content, but the role tags occupy a known absolute position in the wrapped sequence; a linear probe can read tag identity from **positional encoding** alone, without recovering a "role-perception" representation. The standard control — *(Hewitt & Liang 2019)* "Designing and Interpreting Probes with Control Tasks" — is to evaluate probe accuracy under randomized labels (memorization baseline) and **tag-position-shuffled inputs** (positional confound baseline). Neither appears in the protocol description.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should report probe accuracy on tag-randomization and tag-position-shuffled controls so readers can separate the role-perception signal from positional memorization.

**Presentation.** Figure 1's three-model compliance transcripts (gpt-oss-120b, GPT-5, o4-mini producing harmful output under fake green-shirt policy) are visceral evidence but anecdotal — the reader cannot distinguish **representative cases** from cherry-picked successful runs. The 60% ASR aggregate is reported as a single point estimate per model with no seed variance reported across StrongREJECT prompts.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should add a per-prompt success-rate distribution and identify whether Figure 1's transcripts are sampled from typical or maximally-compliant runs.

**Significance.** The headline finding — **CoT Forgery** succeeds *most* on the strongest reasoning models — is an **inverse-scaling** result *(McKenzie et al. 2023)* in safety: as users adopt newer reasoning-tuned models (GPT-5, o4-mini, gpt-oss-120b), the role-confusion failure mode becomes *more* exploitable. This deployment urgency is implicit in the empirical pattern but not foregrounded as an inverse-scaling law. Without the framing, downstream readers may underestimate the interaction between reasoning-capability scaling and adversarial robustness.
**Recommendation:** the authors should explicitly position CoT Forgery within the inverse-scaling literature and report ASR as a function of model reasoning-capability metric where data permits.

**Originality.** The role-probes-on-residual-stream methodology has a direct prior in **Instruction Hierarchy** training *(Wallace et al. 2024)* — the IH paper trained models to differentiate privileged vs. attacker channels, exactly the architectural distinction the role probes are now used to *measure*. The paper engages the behavioral fragility literature (Wang et al., 2025b) but does not position the role-probe contribution as an interpretability companion to IH training, leaving a useful framing unstated.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should engage with Wallace et al. (2024) Instruction Hierarchy as the structured-prompt training prior and frame role probes as the interpretability handle for IH-trained models.
