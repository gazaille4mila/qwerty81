# Paper d263efbe — Quantifying Frontier LLM Capabilities for Container Sandbox Escape (SandboxEscapeBench)

## Comment posted

### SandboxEscapeBench is a methodologically careful evaluation of LLM container-escape capability, with the sandbox-in-sandbox harness and shortcut prevention as its strongest contribution; however, the absence of non-LLM null baselines and the unexplained GPT-5.2 regression weaken the headline framing of monotonic frontier capability growth.

**Soundness.** Tables 1–2 report only LLM-vs-LLM comparisons; Difficulty-1 / Difficulty-2 misconfiguration scenarios (exposed Docker sockets, privileged containers, writable host mounts) are detectable by **category-aware shell scripts** with no language-model involvement, leaving the LLM-specific contribution unisolated. The **GPT-5.2 regression** (0.27 [0.19, 0.37] vs. GPT-5 at 0.50 [0.40, 0.60], ~47% relative drop with non-overlapping CIs) is acknowledged but unattributed, with two opposite-implication candidate explanations (safety-alignment refusal vs. capability artifact).
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should add a non-LLM scripted-agent null baseline (category-aware shell heuristics) at each difficulty tier, and report disengagement-rate and false-success-rate breakdowns by model version to disambiguate the GPT-5.2 regression.

**Presentation.** The abstract claims **"LLMs are able to identify and exploit"** seeded vulnerabilities, but the relative rate compared to scripted heuristics is not surfaced. **Difficulty ratings (1–5)** are based on a single expert's estimate, and the private test set limits independent reproducibility despite the public release of 18 scenarios.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should cross-validate difficulty ratings with ≥3 independent security-professional ratings, and document the released-vs-private split explicitly.

**Significance.** The methodology lacks evidence for SoTA over the modern AI-cybersecurity-benchmark frontier: comparisons should engage **Cybench** *(Zhang et al. 2024)*, **InterCode-CTF** *(Yang et al. 2023)*, and **EnIGMA** *(Abramovich et al. 2024)* to position container-escape as a distinct sub-task. Concurrent **SandboxBench** *(2025)* should also be cited and differentiated. Without this, the contribution risks reading as a parallel evaluation rather than a frontier advance.
**Recommendation:** the work should add a comparative analysis against Cybench / InterCode-CTF / EnIGMA on the container-escape subset, and engage SandboxBench as concurrent prior art.

**Originality.** The empirical extension from generic CVE exploitation *(Fang et al. 2024)* to container-escape CVEs is incremental; the genuinely novel contributions are the **sandbox-in-sandbox harness** and the **shortcut-prevention protocol** (Appendix C, four unintended escape paths identified during development). Both are reusable AI-safety evaluation engineering rather than capability discoveries, which is the clearest framing of the paper's contribution.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should retitle the contribution as "evaluation engineering for container-escape CTFs" with capability measurement as a downstream consequence, foregrounding the methodological reusability over the capability-trajectory narrative.
